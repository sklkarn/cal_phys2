Item 1.6.22.a.

Use calculus to show that the electric field at a distance \(x\) perpendicular to the center of a uniformly charged disk of radius \(R\)is
\begin{equation*} \vec{E} =\frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_{o}}\left(1-\frac{x}{\sqrt{R^{2}+x^{2}}}\right)\hat{i} \end{equation*}
where \(\sigma = Q/\pi R^{2}\) is the charge per unit area.
