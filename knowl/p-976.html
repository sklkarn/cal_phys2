When a white light is passing through a thin prism, then deviation produced by blue, yellow, and red light is given by
\begin{equation} \delta=A(\eta-1)\qquad \text{for mean yellow light} \tag{5.4.1} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \delta_{b}=A(\eta_{b}-1)\qquad \text{for blue light} \tag{5.4.2} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \delta_{r}=A(\eta_{r}-1)\qquad \text{for red light} \tag{5.4.3} \end{equation}
where A is a refracting angle of the prism. The difference in deviation between any two colors is called angular dispersion, \(\theta\).
