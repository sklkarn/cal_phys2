Proof

The magnetic field at a point P on the circular path due to current carrying wire is given by
\begin{equation*} B=\frac{\mu_{o}I}{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
Magnetic field is parallel to the tangential direction at any point on circular path. Hence line integral of the field around the circular path centered on the current carrying wire is given by
\begin{equation*} \oint\vec{B}\cdot\vec{\,dl} = \oint B\,dl\cos\theta = \oint B \,dl \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\oint \frac{\mu_{o}I}{2\pi r} (r\,d\theta)= \frac{\mu_{o}I}{2\pi} (2\pi)=\mu_{o}I \end{equation*}
Thus we have -
\begin{equation*} \oint\vec{B}\cdot\vec{dl} = \mu_{o} I_{enc} \end{equation*}
