The dispersion produced by the prisms are given by
\begin{equation*} \delta_{b}-\delta_{r} =A(\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad \delta'_{b}-\delta'_{r} =A'(\eta'_{b}-\eta'_{r}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{Net angular dispersion} = (\delta_{b}-\delta_{r})+(\delta'_{b}-\delta'_{r}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = A(\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}) + A'(\eta'_{b}-\eta'_{r}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \text{Net angular dispersion} = A\left[(\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}) + \frac{A'}{A}(\eta'_{b}-\eta'_{r})\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =A\left[(\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}) - \frac{(\eta-1)}{(\eta'-1)}(\eta'_{b}-\eta'_{r})\right] \end{equation*}
\(\therefore\) Net angular dispersion = \(A(\eta-1)\left[\frac{(\eta_{b}-\eta_{r})}{(\eta-1)} - \frac{(\eta'_{b}-\eta'_{r})}{(\eta'-1)}\right]\)
\begin{equation*} = A(\eta-1)(\omega'-\omega) \end{equation*}
where \(\omega\) and \(\omega'\) are the dispersive powers of the materials of the prisms. If two prisms are of the same materials, then \(\eta =\eta' \) and \(\omega=\omega'\text{.}\) So, A = Aâ€™ from from eqn. (5.3.9) hence, no dispersion and no deviation.
