Consider two straight conductors of length L carry current \(I_{1}\) and \(I_{2}\) are lying parallel to each other at a distance r. The magnetic field exerted by the current \(I_{1}\) at a distance r is given by
\begin{equation*} B_{1}=\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}}{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
into the page. The direction of field can be determined by right hand thumb rule.
