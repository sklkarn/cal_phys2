Solution 1.3.11.1.

Consider a ring of radius a having total charge Q. To find the field at point P consider an elementary charge \(\,dq \) then field at P is given by
\begin{equation*} \,dE=\frac{k\,dq}{r^{2}} \end{equation*}
Because of the charge symmetry, y components of field cancel out each other and only x components survived. Hence,
\begin{equation*} \,dE_{x} =\,dE\cos\theta=\frac{k\,dq}{r^{2}}\cos\theta = \frac{k\,dq}{r^{2}} \frac{x}{r} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{k\,dq}{r^{3}} = \frac{k x \,dq}{(x^{2}+a^{2})^3/2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore \quad E= \int\,dE_{x} =\int \frac{k x \,dq}{(x^{2}+a^{2})^3/2} = \frac{k x Q}{(x^{2}+a^{2})^3/2} \tag{1.3.1} \end{equation}
