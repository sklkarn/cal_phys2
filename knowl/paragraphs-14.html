1.

Current passes through a solution of sodium chloride. In two seconds, \(4.60\times10^{16} \,Na^{+} \) ions arrive at the negative electrode, and \(3.00\times10^{16} \,Cl^{-}\) arrive at the positive electrode.
  1. What is the current passing through the electrodes?
  2. If the voltage across the electrodes is 3 V, what is the effective resistance of the solution?
  3. How much electrical power is converted to heat during one minute of operation?

2.

An aluminum wire is 10 m long, 0.4 mm in radius, and contains an electric field of 0.5 V/m. Find
  1. the current in the wire,
  2. the resistance of the wire,
  3. the power dissipated by the wire,
  4. the potential difference between the ends of the wire, and
  5. the current density in the wire.
NOTE: \(\rho_{al} = 2.75\times10^{-8} \,\Omega.m, \quad D_{al} = 2.7 \,g/cm^{3}, \) \(M_{al} = 26.98 \,g/mole.\)

3.

The potential difference between points in a wire 10 m apart is 12 V when the current density is \(4\times10^{7} \,A/m^{2}.\) If the wire has a radius of \(2.0\times10^{-5} \,m,\) find
  1. the electric field in the wire,
  2. the resistivity of the material in the wire,
  3. the resistance of the wire,
  4. the current in the wire, and
  5. the power dissipated in the wire.

4.

  1. What is the resistance of a copper wire of radius \(1.0\times 10^{-4} \,m \) and length \(2.0\times 10^{3} \,m?\)
  2. How much energy is converted into heat when this wire is connected to a 12 V battery for 60 s?
  3. What is the mass of the wire?
  4. How much does the temperature of the wire increase (assuming no heat loss to the surroundings)?
  5. How much does the resistance of the wire increase due to this heat?
NOTE: For copper \(\alpha = 0.00393 \,/^{o}C, \quad \rho = 1.72\times10^{-8} \,\Omega.m, \) \(D = 8.9 \,g/cm^3, \) and \(c = 390 \,J/kg.K.\)

5.

A carbon resistor is to be used as a thermometer. On a winter day when the temperature is freezing, its resistance is \(237.5 \,\Omega.\)
  1. What is the temperature on a hot summer day when the resistance is \(233.9 \,\Omega?\)
  2. What current flows through the resistor in the winter if the ohmmeter uses a 1.5 V battery?
  3. How much power does the resistor dissipate in the winter if it takes 5 seconds to measure its resistance?
NOTE: For carbon \(\alpha = -0.0005 \,/^{o}C\text{,}\) \(\rho = 3.5\times10^{-5} \,\Omega.m, \) \(D = 2.25 \,g/cm^{3}.\)
