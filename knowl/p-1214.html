  1. As I reflected ray is \(180^{o}\) out of phase by itself due to reflection from a surface of high refracting medium, so in order to make it interferes constructively with II emergent ray from films the total path difference between these rays must be equal to integral multiple of \(\lambda/2\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} \therefore \quad 2t=m\frac{\lambda_{film}}{2}, \end{equation*}
    set m = 1 for minimum thickness.
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad 2t=\frac{\lambda_{film}}{2} \quad \text{for bright fringe} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} but, n = \frac{c}{v}= \frac{\nu \lambda_{o}}{\nu \lambda_{film}} = \frac{ \lambda_{o}}{ \lambda_{film}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \Rightarrow \lambda_{film} = \frac{\lambda_{o}}{n_{2}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad t=\frac{\lambda_{film}}{4} = \frac{\lambda_{o}}{4 n_{2}} = \frac{650 \,nm}{4\times1.33} =122 \,nm \end{equation*}
  2. As I and II rays are \(180^{o}\) out of phase due to reflection from a surface of high refracting medium, so in order to make them interfere destructively total path difference between them must be equal to integral multiple of \(\lambda/2\)
    \begin{equation*} \therefore 2t = m\frac{\lambda_{film}}{2} \end{equation*}
    set m = 1$ for minimum thickness.
    \begin{equation*} \text{or},\quad t = \frac{\lambda_{film}}{4} = \frac{\lambda_{o}}{4 n_{2}} = \frac{550\,nm}{4\times1.4} =98\,nm \end{equation*}
  3. Since green light is transmitted, red and blue are reflected. Therefore, color reflected is magenta.
