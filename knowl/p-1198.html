In FigureÂ 5.8.12.(b), the red envelop represents the intensity distribution due to diffraction in double slits \(\left[\frac{\sin\alpha}{\alpha} \right]^{2},\) also called diffraction factor and blue curve represents the intensity distribution due to interference of waves from both the slits \(\left[\cos^{2}\beta\right],\) also called interference factor.
\begin{equation*} \text{Here}\quad \beta = \frac{\pi d\sin\theta}{\lambda} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad \alpha =\frac{\pi a\sin\theta}{\lambda} \end{equation*}
Interference maxima occur when \(\,d\sin\theta =m\lambda\) and first diffraction minimum occurs when \(\,a\sin\theta =\lambda\text{.}\) The \(m^{th} \) order of interference maximum coincide with the first order diffraction minimum, which is given by \(m=d/a\text{.}\) If \(\frac{d}{a}=\frac{a+b}{a} =1;\quad b=0\text{.}\) In this case, the first order spectrum will be absent and the resultant diffraction pattern is similar to single slit diffraction. If \(\frac{a+b}{a} =2; \quad a=b\text{,}\) then second order spectrum will be absent.
