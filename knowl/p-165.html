The electrical field within a continuous distribution of charge is given by the equation
\begin{equation*} \vec{E} = cx\hat{i} + cy\hat{j} + cz\hat{k}, \end{equation*}
where \(c = 1\,N/C.m\) is a constant, (x,y,z) are Cartesian coordinates, and (i,j,k) are the Cartesian unit vectors.
  1. Write the equation for \(\vec{E}\) in terms of the position vector \(\vec{r}\text{.}\) What kind of symmetry is associated with this function?
  2. What is the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius 1 m centered at the origin of the coordinates? How much electrical charge is located inside this sphere?
  3. What is the electric flux through a cube 1 m on a side centered at the origin of the coordinates? How much electrical charge is located inside this cube?
