\begin{equation*} or,\quad y(x,t)= A\left[2\sin\frac{(kx-\omega t)+ (kx+\omega t)}{2}\cos\frac{(kx-\omega t)- (kx+\omega t)}{2}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad y(x,t) = 2A\sin kx\cos\omega t\tag{4.4.6} \end{equation}
The term \(\sin kx \) describes the sinusoidal shape of the wave pattern and the term \(\cos\omega t\) describes the flip-flop (up-down oscillatory) motion of each wave segment. This is no longer a progressive wave equation as position and time dependence of the wave equation have been separated from each other. The term \(2A \,\sin kx \) defines the amplitude of a wave at a given location and the term \(\cos\omega t\) defines the variation in displacement of a medium particle with time, t.
