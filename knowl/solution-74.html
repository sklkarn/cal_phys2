Solution 5.3.2.1.

From figure
\begin{equation*} \alpha+\theta =90^{o} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} also,\quad \alpha+y =90^{o} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore,\quad y =90^{o}-\alpha =90^{o}-(90^{o}-\theta) =\theta \end{equation*}
similarly, \(x=\theta\)
But in \(\bigtriangleup OQR,\)
\begin{equation*} \theta+x+y=180^{o} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta = 60^{o} \end{equation*}
