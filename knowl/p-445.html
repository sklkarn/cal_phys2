Due to symmetry of current along the ring y components of field cancel out but x component survived, hence
\begin{equation*} \,dB\sin\phi=\frac{\mu_{o}I}{4\pi}\frac{\,dl}{r^{2}}\frac{a}{r} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{\mu_{o}I}{4\pi}\frac{\,dl a}{r^{3}} = \frac{\mu_{o}I}{4\pi} \frac{a\,dl}{(a^{2}+x^{2})^{3/2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad B= \int\,dB = \int\limits_{0}^{2\pi a}\frac{\mu_{o}I}{4\pi} \frac{a\,dl}{(a^{2}+x^{2})^{3/2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{\mu_{o}I}{4\pi} \frac{a}{(a^{2}+x^{2})^{3/2}} \left[l\right]_{0}^{2\pi a} = \frac{\mu_{o}I}{4\pi} \frac{a (2\pi a)}{(a^{2}+x^{2})^{3/2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} B= \frac{\mu_{o}I}{2} \frac{a^{2}}{(a^{2}+x^{2})^{3/2}} \end{equation*}
If number of turns in a circular loop is N, then
\begin{equation} B=\frac{\mu_{o}I N a^{2}}{2(a^{2}+x^{2})^{3/2}} \tag{3.4.2} \end{equation}
along the x-axis.
