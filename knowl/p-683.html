The equation of SHM can be represented equivalently by
\begin{equation*} y=A \cos(\omega t\pm \phi_{o}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad y = A\sin \left(\omega t\pm \phi_{o}\right) \end{equation*}
The value of \(\phi_{o} \) depends upon the time from which it is counted. Let us consider the reference circle FigureÂ 4.1.2 and time is counted from the point S above the mean position, then phase angle of the particle is
\begin{equation*} \left(\omega t + \phi_{o}\right), \end{equation*}
if time is counted from point \(S'\) below the mean position, then phase angle of the particle is
\begin{equation*} \left(\omega t - \phi_{o}\right), \end{equation*}
and if time is counted from the point when particle is just at the mean position, then phase angle of the particle is \(\left(\omega t \right).\) This equation can also be the combination of both or in more general in a complex form such as
\begin{equation*} y=A \sin(\omega t + \phi_{o}) + B \cos(\omega t+ \phi_{o}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad y = Ae^{i(kt+\phi_{o})} \end{equation*}
