Atomic Mass Unit, amu or u: 1 amu is \(\frac{1}{12}\) of the mass of C-12 atom. Mass of one atom of C-12 is 12 amu as it contains 12 (6 proton + 6 neutrons) elementary particles. Since \(6.022\times10^{23}\) atoms of C-12 has mass of 12 g, hence
\begin{equation*} 1 \,amu = \left(\frac{1}{12}\right) \, (\text{mass of one atom of C-12}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \left(\frac{1}{12}\right)\left(\frac{12\,g/mol}{6.022\times10^{23}\,1/mol}\right) = 1.66\times10^{-24} \,g = 1\,u. \end{equation*}
It is the mass of one proton or one hydrogen atom.
