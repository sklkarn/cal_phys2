Let us consider an elastic string tightly stretched between its two ends by a constant tension T. The string is caused to vibrate by plucking it with a very small displacement so that the tension T remains constant through out the deflections of the string. Consider a small element of the string of arc length \(\,ds\) [FigureÂ 4.4.1] which is moving at a constant velocity, v towards right. If this arc subtends an angle \(2\theta\) at its center then length of arc, \(\,ds=R (2\theta)\) where R is radius of the arc. If \(\mu \) is mass per unit length of a string then mass of the arc,
\begin{equation*} m = \mu R 2\theta. \end{equation*}
The force acting on the string element along x-axis is given by
\begin{equation*} F_{x}= T\cos\theta-T\cos\theta=0, \end{equation*}
i.e., there is no horizontal motion of the particle of a string.
