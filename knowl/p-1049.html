  1. \begin{equation*} n=\frac{c}{v} \Rightarrow v=\frac{c}{n} =\frac{3 \times10^{8} \,m/s}{1.4}= 2.14\times 10^{8} \,m/s \end{equation*}
  2. Frequency does not change as the wave goes from one medium to another. Therefore \(f_{a}=f_{b}=6.0 \times 10^{14} \,Hz.\)
  3. \begin{equation*} v=f\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda = \frac{v}{f}= \frac{2.14\times 10^{8} \,m/s}{6.0 \times 10^{14}\, Hz} = 356 \,nm \end{equation*}
  4. \begin{equation*} \lambda = \frac{c}{f} = \frac{3 \times10^{8} \,m/s}{8.50 \times 10^{14} \,Hz} = 352 \,nm \quad (UV light.) \end{equation*}
