Now consider a small cylindrical Gaussian surface CD having the base C passing through P, the other side D lying inside the conductor and the curved surface being perpendicular to the surface of the conductor. The flux through the curved surface is zero since there is no component of electric field normal to the curved surface. Also the flux through the base D is zero as electric field inside the conductor is zero. Therefore the resultant flux of the entire surface of the Gaussian cylinder is the same as the flux through the face C which is equal to
\begin{equation*} \phi=\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,ds} =E\,ds=\frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad E\,ds = \frac{\sigma\,ds}{\epsilon_{o}}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
