\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}q}{\,dt^{2}} + 2\lambda\frac{\,dq}{\,dt}+\omega^{2}_{o}\left[q-\frac{E}{L\omega^{2}_{o}}\right] =0 \tag{3.7.9} \end{equation}
\begin{equation*} \text{Let}\quad \left[q-\frac{E}{L\omega^{2}_{o}}\right]=x \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{so that}\quad \frac{\,dq}{\,dt} =\frac{\,dx}{\,dt} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}q}{\,dt^{2}} =\frac{\,d^{2}x}{\,dt^{2}} \end{equation*}
Therefore from eqn. (3.7.9) we have -
\begin{equation} \frac{\,d^{2}x}{\,dt^{2}}+2\lambda\frac{\,dx}{\,dt}+\omega^{2}_{o}x =0 \tag{3.7.10} \end{equation}
Which is a second order linear differential equation.
