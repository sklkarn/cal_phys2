On the basis of particle motion relative to the direction of energy propagation there are two forms of waves
  1. Longitudinal wave, and
  2. Transverse wave.
In longitudinal wave the medium particles vibrate parallel to the direction of the energy propagation. Longitudinal waves are also called pressure waves. As a layer of one part of a medium creates pressure on the other part of the medium. Sound waves in any medium, waves propagating along spring are some examples of such wave. In transverse wave the medium particles vibrate perpendicular to the direction of the energy propagation. Movement of a wave through a solid object like a stretched rope or a trampoline is some examples of such wave.
