Here B varies along z-axis, so \(B_{x}\) is replaced with \(B_{z}\) but its direction is along +x axis above top surface and along -x axis below bottom surface of the sheet.
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad B_{z} = \frac{\mu_{o}I_{enc}}{2\Delta x}= \frac{\mu_{o}K}{2} \end{equation*}
for any \(z \gt 0.\) Hence,
\begin{equation*} B(x,y,z)=\begin{cases} +\frac{\mu_{o}K}{2}\hat{x} & \text{for} \quad z \gt 0\\ -\frac{\mu_{o}K}{2}\hat{x} & \text{for} \quad z \lt 0 \end{cases} \end{equation*}
Note that the field is completely uniform above the sheet, and likewise uniform below the sheet, but across the sheet there is discontinuity.
