\begin{equation*} \text{In loop I,}\quad 0 = I_1 P+I_1 G +I_1 R-I_2 G-I_3 R \end{equation*}
In loop I [\(I_1\) is + ve], hence \(I_2\) and \(I_3\) are taken as - ve as they oppose the current \(I_1\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \text{In loop II,}\quad 0 = I_2 Q+I_2 X +I_2 G -I_1 G-I_3 X \end{equation*}
In loop II [\(I_2\) is +ve], hence \(I_1\) and \(I_3\) are taken as - ve as they oppose the current \(I_2\text{.}\) Since Wheat-stone bridge is used at balanced condtion no current should pass through the Galvanometer, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} I_1+I_2=0 \end{equation*}
Hence, in this case
\begin{equation*} I_1+(-I_2)=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or, \quad I_1 = I_2 \end{equation*}
Now from above equations we have
\begin{equation*} I_1 (P + R) = I_3 R \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or, \quad I_3=\frac{(P + R) }{R}I_1 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad I_2 (Q + X) = I_3 X \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or, \quad I_3=\frac{(Q + X) }{X}I_2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{X} \end{equation*}
\([\because I_1=I_2]\)
