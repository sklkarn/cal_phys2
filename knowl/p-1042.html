Remember higher curvature means smaller radius of curvature. For the glass lens,
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{1}}=(^{a}n_{g}-1) \left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}} \right) = (1.5-1) \left(\frac{1}{10}-\frac{1}{20}\right) = \frac{1}{40} \end{equation*}
For the oil lens,
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{2}}=(^{a}n_{o}-1) \left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}} \right) = (1.6-1) \left(\frac{1}{\infty}+\frac{1}{20}\right) = \frac{3}{100} \end{equation*}
Therefore the equivalent focal length of the combination is given by,
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{F}=\frac{1}{f_{1}}+\frac{1}{f_{2}}= \frac{1}{40}+\frac{3}{100} = \frac{11}{200} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad F = 18.18 \,cm \end{equation*}
