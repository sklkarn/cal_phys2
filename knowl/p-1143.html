Let a point of equal intensity of illumination be at a distance \(R_{1} =r\) from the source \(L_{1},\) then
\begin{equation*} \frac{L_{1}}{R_{1}^{2}} = \frac{L_{2}}{R_{2}^{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or, \quad \frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}} = \left(\frac{R_{1}}{R_{2}}\right)^{2} = \left(\frac{r}{1-r}\right)^{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad r = 2,\, 2/3 \end{equation*}
Since sources are placed at 1 m apart, r must be 2/3 m.
