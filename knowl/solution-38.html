Solution 2.5.2.1.

  1. For first figure
    \begin{equation*} E = 6 \,V;\quad r = 10 \,\Omega;\quad R= 4000 \,\Omega \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} R_{T} = r +R; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} V_{T} = I R_{T}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} V = I R \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} R_{T} = 4010 \,\Omega; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} I = 1.496 \,mA; \end{equation*}
    Hence, Ammeter reads 1.496 mA.
    \begin{equation*} V = 5.985 \,V; \end{equation*}
    Hence, Voltmeter reads 5.985 V.
  2. For second figure [SubsectionÂ 2.3.4] No current flows. Hence, Ammeter reads 0 current and Voltmeter reads 6 V.
  3. For third figure No current flows. Hence, Ammeter reads 0 current and Voltmeter reads 6 V.
Note: Ammeter is a negligibly low internal resistance device. It could not appreciably affect any current that is flowing in the sircuit. Hence it is always used as a series connection in the circuit.
Voltmeter is an infinitely high internal resistance device. It could not allow any current flows through it from the circuit. Hence it is always used as a parallel connection in the circuit element at which we are measuring the voltage.
