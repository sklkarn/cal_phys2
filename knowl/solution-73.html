Solution 5.3.1.1.

Consider a person, HF standing in front of a plane mirror, MN. He can see his full image only if the light rays from his feet to head after reflected from the mirror fall on his eye, E.
From the law of reflection
\begin{equation*} i =\angle HMQ = r = \angle QME \end{equation*}
Since MN is parallel to HF, \(\triangle^{s} HMQ\) and \(QME\) are similar and hence the ratio of their corresponding sides are equal, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \frac{HQ}{QE} = \frac{MQ}{MQ}\quad \Rightarrow HQ = QE \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad HE=HE+QE= 2QE \tag{5.3.1} \end{equation}
Similarly, from similar \(\triangle^{s} ENR\) and \(RNF\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad EF=ER+RF= 2ER \tag{5.3.2} \end{equation}
From eqns. (5.3.1) and (5.3.2), we get -
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad HF=HE+EF= 2QE + 2ER = 2(QE+ER) \tag{5.3.3} \end{equation}
If EP is drawn perpendicular on MN from E, then the lines MQ, PE, and NR are all parallel to each other, hence,
\begin{equation*} MP=QE\qquad \text{also}\quad PN=ER \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\qquad HF = 2(QE+ER) = 2(MP+PN) = 2MN \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} or, \quad MN= \frac{1}{2}HF\tag{5.3.4} \end{equation}
Hence, the size of mirror, MN must be half the size of a person, HF.
