Consider a charged q at the geometric center of a cylinder of radius R and length l. An enclosed charge emits fluxes outward through all of its surfaces. To find the flux through the top and bottom caps of the cylinder let us consider an elementary ring of radius r and thickness \(\,dr\) then flux is given by
\begin{equation*} \,d\phi = \vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,ds}= E\,ds \cos\theta = \frac{kq}{\left(r^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}\right)}(2\pi r\,dr)\cos\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \phi_{top\hspace{2pt} cap} =\int\,d\phi=\int\limits_{0}^{\alpha}\frac{kq}{\left(r^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}\right)}(2\pi r\,dr)\cos\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{but}\quad r=\frac{l}{2}\tan\theta \quad \text{or,}\quad \,dr=\frac{l}{2}\sec^{2}\theta\,d\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \phi_{top\hspace{2pt} cap} = \int\limits_{0}^{\alpha}\frac{kq}{\left(\frac{l^{2}}{4}\tan^{2}\theta+\frac{l^{2}}{4}\right)}(2\pi \frac{l}{2}\tan\theta \frac{l}{2}\sec^{2}\theta\,d\theta)\cos\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =2\pi kq \int\limits_{0}^{\alpha}\sin\theta \,d\theta =2\pi kq\left[-\cos\theta\right]_{0}^{\alpha} = 2\pi kq\left[1-\cos\alpha\right] \end{equation*}
But, from the geometry of cylinder,
\begin{equation*} \cos\alpha=\frac{\frac{l}{2}}{\sqrt{\frac{l^{2}}{4}+R^{2}}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \phi_{top\hspace{2pt} cap} = 2\pi kq\left[1-\frac{l}{2\sqrt{R^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}}}\right]\ \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{similarly,}\quad \phi_{bottom\hspace{2pt} cap} = 2\pi kq\left[1-\frac{l}{2\sqrt{R^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}}}\right]; \end{equation*}
But, from Gaussâ€™s law
\begin{equation*} \phi_{total} = \frac{q}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
Therefore, the flux through the curved surface =
\begin{equation*} \phi_{curved} = \phi_{total} - \left( \phi_{top\hspace{2pt} cap} + \phi_{bottom\hspace{2pt} cap}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \phi_{curved} = \frac{q}{\epsilon_{o}}-4\pi kq \left[1-\frac{l}{2\sqrt{R^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}}}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{q}{\epsilon_{o}}-\frac{4\pi q}{4\pi \epsilon_{o}} \left[\frac{2\sqrt{R^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}}-l}{2\sqrt{R^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}}}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \phi_{curved} = \frac{q}{\epsilon_{o}}\left[1-1+\frac{l}{2\sqrt{R^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}}}\right] = \frac{ql}{2\epsilon_{o}\sqrt{R^{2}+\frac{l^{2}}{4}}} \end{equation*}
