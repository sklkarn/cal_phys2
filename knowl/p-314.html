That is, power transfer depends upon the load resistance. Hence, maximum power transfer can be obtained by
\begin{align*} \frac{dP}{dR} \amp =0 \\ or, \quad \frac{d}{dR}\left[\frac{E^{2}R}{(R+r)^{2}}\right]\amp =0\\ or, \quad \frac{E^{2}}{(R+r)^{2}}+R\left[E^{2}\left(\frac{-2}{(R+r)^{3}}\right).1\right] \amp =0\\ or, \quad \frac{E^{2}}{(R+r)^{2}}\left[1-\frac{2R}{R+r}\right]\amp =0 \end{align*}
\begin{equation*} \because \frac{E}{R+r}=I \neq 0 \qquad \Rightarrow 1-\frac{2R}{R+r} = 0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad R=r \end{equation*}
Thus the source can transfer maximum power to the load only when load resistance is equal to the internal resistance of the source.
