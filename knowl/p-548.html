Discharging of a capacitor When circuit is connected to point 2 capacitor begins discharging through the inductor and resistor. Hence
\begin{equation*} \frac{q}{C}+L\frac{\,di}{\,dt}+Ri=0 \end{equation*}
Since \(i=\pm \frac{\,dq}{\,dt}\) and during discharging \(\,dq \) is decreasing or \(\,dq\) is negative, Therefore \(i=\frac{\,dq}{\,dt}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad L\frac{\,d^{2}q}{\,dt^{2}}+R\frac{\,dq}{\,dt}+\frac{q}{C} =0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}q}{\,dt^{2}}+\frac{R}{L}\frac{\,dq}{\,dt}+\frac{q}{LC} =0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}q}{\,dt^{2}}+2\lambda\frac{\,dq}{\,dt}+\omega^{2}_{o}q =0\tag{3.7.16} \end{equation}
where \([\frac{R}{L} = 2\lambda \) and \(\frac{1}{LC}=\omega^{2}_{o}] \) If \(q=Ae^{\alpha t}\) be the trial solution of eqn. (3.7.16) then this equation becomes
\begin{equation*} \alpha^{2}+2\lambda \alpha +\omega^{2}_{o} =0 \end{equation*}
