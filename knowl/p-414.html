Consider a rectangular current loop ABCDA of surface area A, side length = L, and side width = W is placed in an external magnetic field B [FigureÂ 3.2.3.(b)] so that xz plane is parallel to the field, then the torque acting on the loop is given by
\begin{equation*} \tau = r F = \frac{L}{2}F_{out} + \frac{L}{2}F_{in} \end{equation*}
Where, \(r = OA= \frac{L}{2}, \) the perpendicular distance of \(F_{out}\) or \(F_{in}\) from the axis of rotation. Force along sides EA and BD is zero as \(\vec{B}\) and current element \(Il\) is parallel to each other. Force \(F_{out}\) is coming out of the page along +ve y-axis and \(F_{in}\) is going into the page along -ve y-axis.
\begin{equation*} \tau = \frac{L}{2}\left[F_{out} + F_{in}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{But,}\quad F = IlB \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \tau = \frac{L}{2}\left[IWB + IWB\right] \end{equation*}
here \(l=W\) width of the rectangular plane. Hence,
\begin{equation*} \tau= \frac{L}{2}\left[2IWB\right] = ILWB = IAB = \mu B \end{equation*}
Due to this torque xz-plane rotates about z-axis till it orient parallel to yz-plane.
