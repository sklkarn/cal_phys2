A hydrogen atom consists of a proton and an electron. If the radius of the atom is equal to the Bohr radius of \(0.5292 \times 10^{-10} \,m\text{,}\) what is
  1. the gravitational force between the two particles,
  2. the electrical force between the particles, and
  3. the ratio of the electrical force to the gravitational force?
Note: Newton’s gravitational constant, \(G = 6.67\times 10^{-11} \,N m^{2}/kg^{2};\) Coulomb’s Electrostatic constant, \(k = 9.00 \times 10^{9} \,N m^{2}/C^{2};\) mass of proton, \(m_{p} = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \,kg;\) mass of electron, \(m_{e} = 9.11\times 10^{-31} \,kg;\) charge of proton, \(q_{p} = 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \,C;\) and charge of electron, \(q_{e} = -1.60 \times 10^{-19} \,C.\)
