Item I.

When two convex lenses are in contact:
For lens \(C_{1}\text{,}\) we have-
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{1}} =\frac{1}{s} +\frac{1}{s_{1}} \end{equation*}
For lens \(C_{2}\text{,}\) we have-
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{2}} =\frac{1}{-s_{1}} +\frac{1}{s_{2}} \end{equation*}
Since image \(I_{1}\) behaves as a virtual object for lens \(C_{2}\text{.}\)
Adding both terms we get -
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{1}} +\frac{1}{f_{2}}=\frac{1}{s} +\frac{1}{s_{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{1}{F} = \frac{1}{f_{1}} +\frac{1}{f_{2}} \end{equation*}
A reciprocal of equivalent focal length is a sum of the reciprocal of individual focal lengths of thin lenses being in contact.
