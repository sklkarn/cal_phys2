  1. Series Combination of Capacitors When capacitors are connected in such a way that same charge is seemed to be passing through each capacitor then the combination is called a series combination of capacitors. In this case positive terminal of one capacitor is connected to the negative terminal of another capacitor as shown in Figure 1.7.6.(a). If charge Q is sent in the circuit by a battery of emf, V [Section 2.1], then
    \begin{equation*} V=V_{1} +V_{2}= \frac{Q}{C_{1}}+\frac{Q}{C_{2}} = Q\left[\frac{1}{C_{1}}+\frac{1}{C_{2}}\right] \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation} \frac{V}{Q}= \left[\frac{1}{C_{1}}+\frac{1}{C_{2}}\right]\tag{1.7.2} \end{equation}
    Now replace these capacitors with such a single capacitor that draws a same amount of charge Q from the same battery as shown in Figure 1.7.6.(c), then this new capacitor is called an equivalent capacitor \(C_{eq}\) of all these previous capacitors in series.
    \begin{equation} V= \frac{Q}{C_{eq}};\quad \text{or,}\quad \frac{V}{Q}=\frac{1}{C_{eq}} \tag{1.7.3} \end{equation}
    Hence, from eqns. (1.7.2) and (1.7.3), we get -
    \begin{equation} \frac{1}{C_{eq}}= \frac{1}{C_{1}}+ \frac{1}{C_{2}} \tag{1.7.4} \end{equation}
  2. Parallel Combination of Capacitors When capacitors are connected in such a way that charges are distributed among capacitors then the combination is called a parallel combination of capacitors. In this case similar terminals of capacitors are connected together as shown in Figure 1.7.6.(b). If q is the charge sent in the circuit by a battery of emf, V then
    \begin{equation*} q=q_{1}+q_{2}=C_{1}V+C_{2}V = V\left[C_{1}+C_{2}\right] \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation} \therefore\quad \frac{q}{V} = \left[C_{1}+C_{2}\right] \tag{1.7.5} \end{equation}
    Now if these capacitors are replaced by a single capacitor, \(C_{eq}\) such that circuit draws the same amount of charge from the same battery (Figure 1.7.6.(c)), then
    \begin{equation} q = C_{eq}V \quad \text{or,}\quad \frac{q}{V}=C_{eq} \tag{1.7.6} \end{equation}
    Hence from eqns. (1.7.5) and (1.7.6), we get -
    \begin{equation} C_{eq} = C_{1} + C_{2} \tag{1.7.7} \end{equation}
