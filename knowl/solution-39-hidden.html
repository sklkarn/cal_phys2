Use loop currents direction as \(+ve.\)
\begin{equation*} \text{(in loop i_1)} \quad 1 \,\Omega \, I_{1} + 2 \,\Omega \,I_{1} + 3 \,\Omega \,I_{1} - 3 \,\Omega \,I_{2} = 5\,V \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{(in loop i_2)} \quad 3\Omega \,I_{2} - 3 \,\Omega \,I_{1}+ 4 \,\Omega \,I_{2} + 5 \,\Omega \,I_{2} - 5 \,\Omega\,I_{3} = 10 \,V \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{(in loop i_3)} \quad 6\Omega \,I_{3} + 5 \,\Omega \,I_{3}- 5 \,\Omega \,I_{2} = -5 \,V \end{equation*}
Simplified form of the above equations:
\begin{equation} 6 \,I_{1} - 3\, I_{2} + 0\cdot \,I_{3} = 5 \tag{2.5.1} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} - 3 \,I_{1} + 12 \,I_{2} - 5 \,I_{3} = 10\tag{2.5.2} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} 0 \cdot \,I_{1} - 5 \,I_{2} + 11 \,I_{3} = - 5 \tag{2.5.3} \end{equation}
In matrix form:
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & -3 & 0\\ -3 & 12 & -5\\ 0 & -5 & 11 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} I_{1}\\ I_{2}\\ I_{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5\\ 10\\ -5 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \begin{vmatrix} 6 & -3 & 0 : 5\\ -3 & 12 & -5 : 10\\ 0 & -5 & 11 : -5 \end{vmatrix} \end{equation*}
The Determinant of the matrix:
\begin{equation*} D =\begin{Vmatrix} 6 & -3 & 0\\ -3 & 12 & -5\\ 0 & -5 & 11 \end{Vmatrix} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 6[12\times 11 -(-5)(-5)] -(-3)[-3\times 11-0]+0= 543 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} I_{1}=\frac{\begin{Vmatrix} 5 & -3 & 0\\ 10 & 12 & -5\\ -5 & -5 & 11 \end{Vmatrix}}{D} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{5[12\times11-(-5)(-5)]-(-3)[10\times11 -(-5)(-5)]+0}{D} = 1.45\,A \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} I_{2}=\frac{\begin{Vmatrix} 6 & 5 & 0\\ -3 & 10 & -5\\ 0 & -5 & 11 \end{Vmatrix}}{D} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{6[10\times11-(-5)(-5)]-5[-3\times11 -0]+0}{D} = \frac{675}{543} = 1.24\,A \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} I_{3}=\frac{\begin{Vmatrix} 6 & -3 & 5\\ -3 & 12 & 10\\ 0 & -5 & -5 \end{Vmatrix}}{D} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{6[12\times(-5)-(-5)(10)]-(-3)[-3\times(-5) -0]+5[(-3)\times(-5)-0]}{D} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad I_{3}== \frac{60}{543} = 0.11\,A \end{equation*}
  1. \(\displaystyle I_{a} = I_{1} = 1.45\,A;\)
  2. \(\displaystyle I_{b} = I_{2} = 1.24\,A;\)
  3. \(\displaystyle I_{c} = I_{3} = 0.11\,A;\)
  4. \(V_{dc} = -[1I_{1} +2I_{1}]= -4.36\,V\)
