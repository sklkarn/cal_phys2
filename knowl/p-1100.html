Let a point source of light of one candela at the center of an imaginary sphere of radius r. Suppose the total luminous flux = \(\phi\text{,}\) the total solid angle subtends at the center of sphere by the entire surface area of the sphere,
\begin{equation*} \omega=\frac{\text{area of sphere}}{r^{2}} = \frac{4\pi r^{2}}{r^{2}} =4\pi \, st.rad \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or, \quad \text{one lumen} = \frac{\phi}{\omega}=\frac{\phi}{4\pi} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \phi= 4\pi \,lumen \end{equation*}
Lumen is also defined as the flow of light energy per second through 1 square meter of a source of 1 m radius, where a source of 1 candela is placed at the center of curvature.
