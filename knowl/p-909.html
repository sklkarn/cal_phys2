The principle of reversibility of light states that if the path of light is reversed even after suffering from the number of reflections and refractions, it retraces its original path.
\begin{equation*} {}^a\eta_{w}=\frac{\sin\theta_{1}}{\sin\theta_{2}}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} {}^w\eta_{g}=\frac{\sin\theta_{2}}{\sin\theta_{3}} \end{equation*}
From the principle of reversibility -
\begin{equation*} {}^g\eta_{w}=\frac{\sin\theta_{3}}{\sin\theta_{2}}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} {}^w\eta_{a}=\frac{\sin\theta_{2}}{\sin\theta_{1}} \end{equation*}
Multiplying them we get-
\begin{equation*} {}^a\eta_{w}\times {}^w\eta_{g}\times {}^g\eta_{w}\times{}^w\eta_{a} =\frac{\sin\theta_{1}}{\sin\theta_{2}}\times \frac{\sin\theta_{2}}{\sin\theta_{3}}\times \frac{\sin\theta_{3}}{\sin\theta_{2}}\times\frac{\sin\theta_{2}}{\sin\theta_{1}}=1 \end{equation*}
