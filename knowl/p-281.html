The total current density through a given cross-section \(A\) of a conductor is then writen as
\begin{equation*} I = \int\limits_{s} J.\,dA \end{equation*}
Hence current can also be defined as the flux of current density over a given area [Figure 2.0.1]. It is vector quantity. It’s direction is along the direction of flow of current and it’s unit is \(\frac{Ampere}{meter^2}\) or \((A/m^2)\text{.}\)
