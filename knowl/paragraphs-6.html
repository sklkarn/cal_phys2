22.

A ring of charge \(Q\) and radius \(a\) lies in the y, z -plane and is centered on the origin. Its electric field at any point along the x-axis is given by
\begin{equation*} E =k\frac{Qx}{(x^{2}+a^{2})^{3/2}}\hat{i} \end{equation*}
  1. Use calculus to show that the electric field at a distance \(x\) perpendicular to the center of a uniformly charged disk of radius \(R\)is
    \begin{equation*} \vec{E} =\frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_{o}}\left(1-\frac{x}{\sqrt{R^{2}+x^{2}}}\right)\hat{i} \end{equation*}
    where \(\sigma = Q/\pi R^{2}\) is the charge per unit area.
  2. What does this become when \(x \gt \gt R?\)
  3. When \(x \lt \lt R?\)
