A redistribution of charges in a neutral object when a charged object is brought near it is called an electrostatic induction. Neighboring charges which are brought near a neutral object are known as inducing charges, charges developed on a neutral object are known as induced charges. Induced charges closer to the inducing charges are known as bound charges and charges away from inducing charges are known as free charges (FigureÂ 1.1.1).
