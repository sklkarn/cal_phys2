The rate of variation of magnetic field B along x-axis is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{\,dB_{x}}{\,dx}=\frac{\,d}{\,dx}\left[\frac{\mu_{o}NIa^{2}}{2\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{3/2}}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{\mu_{o} NIa^{2}}{2} \left[-\frac{3}{2} \left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{-5/2}\cdot 2x \right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{\,dB_{x}}{\,dx}=-\frac{3}{2}\mu_{o} NIa^{2} \left[ \left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{-5/2}\cdot x \right] \end{equation*}
For uniform variation of \(B_{x}\text{,}\) \(\frac{\,dB_{x}}{\,dx} \) must be constant and \(\frac{\,d^{2}B_{x}}{\,dx^{2}} \) must be zero. Hence,
\begin{equation*} \frac{\,d^{2}B_{x}}{\,dx^{2}}= -\frac{3}{2}\mu_{o} NIa^{2} \left[ \left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{-5/2}-5x^{2}\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{-7/2} \right] = 0 \end{equation*}

Hence overall magnetic field remains uniform over a considerable region on either side of the point P.
