\begin{equation} a\sin\theta_{n}=n\lambda \tag{5.8.10} \end{equation}
where \(\theta_{n}\) gives the direction of \(n^{th}\) minimum, here \(n =1,2,3,\cdots\text{.}\) If, however, the path difference is odd multiple of \(\frac{\lambda}{2}\text{,}\) the direction of secondary maxima can be obtained. In this case,
\begin{equation} a\sin\theta_{n}=(2n+1)\frac{\lambda}{2} \tag{5.8.11} \end{equation}
Thus, the diffraction pattern due to a single slit consists of a central bright maximum at O followed by secondary maxima and minima on both sides. All waves do not interfere constructively for this situation, and so the maximum is not as intense as the central maximum. The intensity distribution on the screen is given in Figure 5.8.10.(b). The secondary maxima are of much less intensity and the intensity also falls of rapidly from the point O outwards. From Figure 5.8.10.(a), we have
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta=\frac{y}{D} \simeq \sin\theta = \frac{\lambda}{a} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \Rightarrow y=\frac{\lambda D}{a} \quad \text{(Small angles)}\tag{5.8.12} \end{equation}
Therefore, the width of central maximum
\begin{equation*} =2y=\frac{2\lambda D}{a}\text{,} \end{equation*}
i.e. no diffraction if width of slit, a is very large as \(\theta\) becomes very small.
\begin{equation*} \text{fringe width,}\quad \beta = y_{2}-y_{1}= D\frac{n\lambda}{a} \quad \text{(small angles.)} \end{equation*}
