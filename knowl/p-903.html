From Figure 5.2.21, \(\angle OAN' =i= \angle COA, \) alternating angles and \(\angle NAE =r= \angle CIA\text{,}\) corresponding angles. Therefore from Snell’s law
\begin{equation*} \eta_{i}\sin\theta_{i} = \eta_{r}\sin\theta_{r} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad \eta_{b}\sin i = 1. \sin r \end{equation*}
where \(\eta_{b}\) is absolute refractive index of water (denser medium) and \(\eta_{a} = 1\) is absolute refractive index of air (rare medium).
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \eta_{b} = \frac{\sin r}{\sin i} \end{equation*}
Since the aperture of eye is very small points A and C are considered very close to each other and hence \(\angle i\) and \(\angle r\) are very small, i.e., \(\sin i \simeq \tan i\) and \(\sin r \simeq \tan r\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \eta_{b} = \frac{\tan r}{\tan i} = \frac{\frac{CA}{CI}}{\frac{CA}{CO}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore \quad \eta_{b} = \frac{CO}{CI} = \frac{\text{Real Depth}}{\text{Apparent Depth}}\tag{5.2.9} \end{equation}
