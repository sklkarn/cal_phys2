Dielectric constant, \(\kappa\) (or Relative Permitivity, \(\epsilon_{r}\)): It is a measure of insulating ability of a medium. It is a ratio of the permitivity of the medium \((\epsilon)\) to the permitivity of a free space \((\epsilon_{o})\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \kappa=\epsilon_{r} =\frac{\epsilon}{\epsilon_{o}}. \end{equation*}
It is an ability of a medium to store electrical energy in presence of electric field.
\begin{equation} \kappa = \frac{\epsilon}{\epsilon_{o}}= \frac{C}{C_{o}}\tag{1.7.9} \end{equation}
