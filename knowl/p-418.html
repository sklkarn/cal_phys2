\begin{equation*} \,dU = -\,dW = \mu B\sin \theta\,d\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad U =\int\,dU= \int\mu B\sin \theta\,d\theta =- \mu B \cos\theta+C_{o} \end{equation*}
Where \(C_{o}\) is the integration constant. When \(U\) is zero at \(\theta=90^{o},\quad C_{o} = 0\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{equation*} U = - \mu B \cos\theta = -\vec{\mu}\cdot\vec{B} \end{equation*}
