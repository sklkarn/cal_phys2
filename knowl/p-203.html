Let us consider a capacitor of capacitance \(C\) has a charge \(q\) stored on it when the potential difference across its plates is \(V \) then
\begin{equation*} V=\frac{q}{C}. \end{equation*}
If a further charge \(\,dq \) is given to the capacitor, then work needs to be done against the potential difference. Hence the work done is,
\begin{equation*} \,dw=V\,dq = \frac{q}{C}\,dq \end{equation*}
Therefore total work done in charging the capacitor from \(0\) to \(Q\) is given by
\begin{equation} W = \int\limits_{0}^{Q} \frac{q}{C}\,dq = \frac{1}{C}\left[\frac{q^{2}}{2}\right]_{0}^{Q} = \frac{Q^{2}}{2C}\tag{1.7.10} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad W = U = \frac{1}{2}QV = \frac{1}{2}CV^{2}\tag{1.7.11} \end{equation}
