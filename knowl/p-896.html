From eqns. (5.2.6) and (5.2.7), we get -
\begin{equation*} \frac{AD}{QS} = \frac{AE}{QR} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{AE}{AD} = \frac{BC}{AD} = \frac{v_{a}t}{v_{b}t} =\frac{QR}{QS} =\frac{v_{a}}{v_{b}} \end{equation*}
Hence, if AD is the radius of the secondary wavefront for the point A, then QS is the radius of the secondary wavefront for the point Q. Let i and r be the angles of incidence and refraction, respectively then from \(\triangle ABC\) and \(ACD\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = \frac{BC/AC}{AD/AC} = \frac{BC}{AD} =\frac{v_{a}t}{v_{b}t} =\frac{v_{a}}{v_{b}} =\frac{\eta_{b}}{\eta_{a}} \end{equation*}
