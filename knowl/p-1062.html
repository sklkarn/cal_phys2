Angle subtended by an object on the eye is known as visual angle. The size of the image formed at retina is proportional to the visual angle. Higher the visual angle larger the apparent size of the object. Consider objects \(O_{1}\) and \(O_{2}\) of different sizes are situated at different distance from the eye but they subtend the same visual angle, \(\theta_{1}\) then size of their images formed on the retina will be equal. Hence they appeared to be of same size.
