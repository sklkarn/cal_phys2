In Figure 5.2.4.(c), \(\angle POL = g,\) is called glancing angle. It is the angle made by the incident ray with the reflecting surface so that \(g+i=90^{o}\text{.}\) Now \(\angle MOQ =g=\angle POL\text{,}\) vertically opposite angle and \(\angle ROM = g.\) Since \(i=r\) and \(r+g=90^{o},\) i.e, \(\angle ROQ = 2g\text{.}\) Once the mirror, LM is rotated by an angle \(\theta\) and takes the position L’M’ then the new glancing is \(g+\theta\) and \(\angle R'OQ = 2(g+\theta)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad R'OR=R'OQ-R OQ = 2(g+\theta) - 2g = 2\theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \angle R'OR = \angle R'OM - \angle ROM \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =(\angle R'OM' + \angle M'OM) - \angle ROM \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = [(g+\theta) +\theta]-g = 2\theta \end{equation*}
