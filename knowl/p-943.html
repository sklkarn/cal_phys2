Consider a convex lens and join the lines coming from its two centers of curvatures at point P at a height y from the optical center, C. If \(\angle LAN=\angle A\) is a refracting angle of a prism at this height of the lens then from quadrilateral \(\square ALPN\text{,}\) we have -
\begin{equation*} \angle A+\angle LPN =180^{o} \end{equation*}
as \(\angle ALP+\angle ANP =180^{o}\text{,}\) AL and AN are tangents on the surfaces of the lens.
