Let us consider a transformer as shown in circuit Figure 3.9.13.(d). Consider \(L_{1}, \quad L_{2}, \quad R_{1},\quad R_{2},\quad N_{1},\) and \(N_{2}\) are inductances, resistances, and number of turns in primary and secondary coils, respectively. Mutual inductance of the two coils is \(M,\) and impedances of the generator and load are \(Z_{g}\) and \(Z_{l}\text{,}\) respectively, then from Kirchhoff’s law -
\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} E=I_{1}\left(Z_{g}+R_{1}+j\omega L_{1}\right) \pm j\omega M I_{2} \\ 0=I_{2}\left(Z_{l}+R_{2}+j\omega L_{2}\right) \pm j\omega M I_{1} \end{cases} \end{equation*}
