Electric field at any point \(P_{1}\) [Figure 1.4.6] outside the cylindrical charge distribution i.e., \(r \gt R\text{:}\) Consider a Gaussian surface of radius \(r\) and length \(l\) symmetric to the charge distribution. Due to symmetry the field \(E_{o}\) is everywhere normal to the curved surface and has the same magnitude at all points of it. There is no contribution in fluxes due to flat bases of the Gaussian surface as \(E_{o}\) is parallel to them. Hence
    \begin{equation*} \phi = \oint\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,ds}= \oint E_{o}\,ds = E_{o}(2\pi rl) \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} or, \quad \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_{o}} = E_{o}(2\pi rl) \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} or, \quad E_{o} = \frac{q_{enc}}{2\pi rl\epsilon_{o}}= \frac{\lambda l}{2\pi \epsilon_{o} rl} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \therefore, \quad E_{o} = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi \epsilon_{o} r} \end{equation*}
    Thus the electric field at outside of a uniform infinite cylindrical charge distribution is same as that due to infinite line charge.
  2. Electric field at the point \(P_{2}\) [Figure 1.4.6] on the surface of cylindrical charge distribution i.e., \(r = R:\)
    \begin{equation*} \phi = \oint\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,ds}= \oint E_{s}\,ds = E_{s}(2\pi R l) = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_{o}} = \frac{\lambda l}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E_{s} = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi \epsilon_{o}R} \end{equation*}
  3. Electric field at the point \(P_{3}\) [Figure 1.4.6] inside the cylindrical charge distribution i.e., \(r \lt R:\)
    \begin{equation*} \phi = \oint\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,ds}= \oint E_{i}\,ds = E_{i}(2\pi r l) = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_{o}} = \frac{\rho \pi r^{2} l}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E_{i} = \frac{\rho r}{2 \epsilon_{o} } = \frac{\lambda r}{2 \pi \epsilon_{o} R^{2}} \end{equation*}
    Thus the electric field inside the cylindrical charge distribution is proportional to the distance of the point from the axis and is directed radially away from the axis. The variation of field with distance \(r\) from the axis of a cylindrical charge is shown in Figure 1.4.3.(c).
