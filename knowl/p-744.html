Harmonics: An instrument that produces the standing wave pattern at specific frequencies (equal to its natural frequencies of vibration) is known as harmonics. Other than a harmonic frequency, the resulting disturbance of the medium is irregular and non-periodic. Harmonics are an integral multiple of fundamental frequency. Harmonics can be expressed as
\begin{equation*} f_{n} = nf_{o},\quad \text{where}\quad n=1,2,3, \cdots. \end{equation*}
The fundamental frequency of vibration is also called a first harmonics.
\begin{equation*} \text{I harmonics}, \quad f_{1} = f_{o}, \quad \text{fundamental frequency}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{II harmonics},\quad f_{2} = 2f_{o}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \quad \text{III harmonics}, f_{2} = 3f_{o}, \end{equation*}
and so on.
