Given a circular loop of current I with a radius a.
  1. Show that the magnetic field at a distance x along the axis of this loop is given by
    \begin{equation*} B = \frac{\mu_{o}Ia^{2}}{2(x^{2}+a^{2})^{3/2}}. \end{equation*}
  2. Use this result to determine the magnetic field along the axis of a coil of N turns.
  3. Use the result of part a to determine the magnetic field at the center of a single loop of current.
  4. What is the magnetic field at the center of a loop of current 10 cm in radius carrying a current of 2 A?
