Item I.

Series Combination of Capacitors When capacitors are connected in such a way that same charge is seemed to be passing through each capacitor then the combination is called a series combination of capacitors. In this case positive terminal of one capacitor is connected to the negative terminal of another capacitor as shown in Figure 1.7.6.(a). If charge Q is sent in the circuit by a battery of emf, V [Section 2.1], then
\begin{equation*} V=V_{1} +V_{2}= \frac{Q}{C_{1}}+\frac{Q}{C_{2}} = Q\left[\frac{1}{C_{1}}+\frac{1}{C_{2}}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \frac{V}{Q}= \left[\frac{1}{C_{1}}+\frac{1}{C_{2}}\right]\tag{1.7.1} \end{equation}
Now replace these capacitors with such a single capacitor that draws a same amount of charge Q from the same battery as shown in Figure 1.7.6.(c), then this new capacitor is called an equivalent capacitor \(C_{eq}\) of all these previous capacitors in series.
\begin{equation} V= \frac{Q}{C_{eq}};\quad \text{or,}\quad \frac{V}{Q}=\frac{1}{C_{eq}} \tag{1.7.2} \end{equation}
Hence, from eqns. (1.7.1) and (1.7.2), we get -
\begin{equation} \frac{1}{C_{eq}}= \frac{1}{C_{1}}+ \frac{1}{C_{2}} \tag{1.7.3} \end{equation}
