In the given figure, d is a lateral shift, BD is an emergent ray, and \(AM=t\) is a thickness of the glass slab. From \(\vartriangle ABP\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \sin\angle PAB=\frac{BP}{AB} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad \sin(i-r)=\frac{d}{AB} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,} \quad d= AB\sin(i-r) \tag{5.2.11} \end{equation}
But from \(\vartriangle AMB\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \cos\angle MAB=\frac{MA}{AB} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad \cos r=\frac{t}{AB} \quad \Rightarrow AB=\frac{t}{\cos r} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,} \quad AB=t\sec r \tag{5.2.12} \end{equation}
Hence from eqns. (5.2.11) and (5.2.12), we get -
\begin{equation} d= tsec r\sin(i-r) \tag{5.2.13} \end{equation}
