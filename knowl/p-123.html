The vector product of two vectors \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\) is denoted by \(\vec{a}\times\vec{b}\) and is defined as
\begin{equation*} \vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \mid{\vec{a}}\mid \mid{\vec{b}}\mid sin\theta \hat {n} = absin\theta {\hat{n}}, \end{equation*}
where \(\theta\) is the angle between \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\text{,}\) and \({\hat{n}}\) is a unit vector perpendicular to the plane made by vectors \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\text{,}\) and is representing the direction of \(\vec{a}\times\vec{b}\text{.}\) Here \(\vec{a}\text{,}\) \(\vec{b}\text{,}\) and \({\hat{n}}\) form a right handed system, as shown in FigureÂ 2.1.4.(a).
