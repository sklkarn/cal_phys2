For the grease spot to be indistinguishable from the paper, we must have -
\begin{equation*} I_{1}(1-x) + I_{2}x =I_{1}x + I_{2}(1-x) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or,\quad I_{1}(1-2x) = I_{2}(1-2x) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad I_{1}=I_{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or,\quad \frac{L_{1}}{r_{1}^{2}} = \frac{L_{2}}{r_{2}^{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}} = \left(\frac{r_{1}}{r_{2}}\right)^{2} \end{equation*}
By measuring the distance \(r_{1}\) and \(r_{2}\) of sources from the grease spot, the luminous intensities of the two sources can be compared. If one of them is known the other can be calculated.
