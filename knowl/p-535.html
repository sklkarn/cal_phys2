Let \(q=Ae^{\alpha t}\) be the solution of eqn. (3.7.5) Where A and \(\alpha\) are constants.
\begin{equation*} \text{Now,}\quad \frac{\,dq}{\,dt}= A\alpha e^{\alpha t} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}q}{\,dt^{2}}= A\alpha^{2} e^{\alpha t} \end{equation*}
Therefore from eqn. (3.7.5), we get -
\begin{equation*} A\alpha^{2} e^{\alpha t}+\omega^{2}Ae^{\alpha t}=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad A e^{\alpha t}\left[\alpha^{2} +\omega^{2}\right]=0 \end{equation*}
\([\text{Since,}\quad q= A e^{\alpha t} \neq 0 ]\)
\begin{equation*} \text{We have -}\quad \left[\alpha^{2} +\omega^{2}\right]=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \alpha=\pm j\omega_{o} \end{equation*}
\([\text{where}\quad j=\sqrt{-1}]\)
