Also pressure fluctuation can be
\begin{equation} p(x,t) = -Bks_{o}\cos(kx-\omega t) \tag{4.4.5} \end{equation}
which is lagging behind the displacement \(s(x,t)\) by quarter of wavelength and
\begin{equation*} p_{max} = B\,k\,s_{o} \end{equation*}
is pressure amplitude.
\begin{equation*} \text{and,}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}s}{\,dx^{2}} =-s_{o}k^{2}\sin(kx-\omega t) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{Also}\quad \frac{\,ds}{\,dt} =-s_{o}\omega\cos(kx-\omega t) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}s}{\,dt^{2}} =-s_{o}\omega^{2}\sin(kx-\omega t) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{Hence,}\quad -s_{o}k^{2}\sin(kx-\omega t) =-\frac{\rho}{B} s_{o}\omega^{2}\sin(kx-\omega t) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad k^{2} =\frac{\rho}{B}\omega^{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{1}{\lambda^{2} f^{2}}=\frac{\rho}{B} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad v^{2}=\frac{B}{\rho} \end{equation*}
Hence the speed of longitudinal wave in fluid is given by
\begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}} \end{equation*}
