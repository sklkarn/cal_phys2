In general speed of longitudinal wave is
\begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{\text{elastic property}}{\text{inertial property}}} \end{equation*}
This is the same as writing
\begin{equation*} v = \sqrt{\frac{Y}{\rho}} \quad \text{(in solid)} \end{equation*}
Newton assumed that the process is very slow when a wave passes through fluid hence the temperature remains constant through out the process, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} PV =constant \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad P\,dV+V\,dP =0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad P\,dV=-V\,dP \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad P = -\frac{\,dP}{\,dV/V} = B \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad v = \sqrt{\frac{P}{\rho}} \end{equation*}
Now at normal temperature and pressure (NTP) the atmospheric pressure is \(P=1.01\times10^{5} \,N/m^{2}\) and density of air is \(\rho=1.293 \,kg/m^{3}\text{,}\) we have velocity of sound in air is
\begin{equation*} v = \sqrt{\frac{P}{\rho}}=280\, m/s \end{equation*}
But the experimental value of sound in air is 332 m/s. Hence Laplace modified this solution by assuming that when wave flows through the fluid compression and rarefaction occur so fast that there is no exchange of heat between the system and surrounding in fluid. Hence
\begin{equation*} PV^{\gamma} = constant \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad V^{\gamma}\,dP + P\gamma V^{\gamma-1}\,dV =0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad V^{\gamma}\,dP =- P\gamma V^{\gamma-1}\,dV \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad \,dP =- P \gamma V^{-1}\,dV \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,} \quad -\frac{\,dP}{\,dV/V} = P \gamma = B \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma P}{\rho}}=332 \,m/s \quad [\gamma=1.4] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{Also,} \quad v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma R T}{M}}\quad \text{(In Ideal Gas)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \left[\because\quad PV = nRT = \frac{m}{M}RT] \quad \text{or,} \quad P = \frac{RT}{\rho \,M}\right]. \end{equation*}
Here \(n, \, M,\) and \(T\) stand for number of moles, molar mass, and absolute temperature of gas, respectively.
