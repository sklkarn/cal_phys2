Charge Density: Depend upon the surface where charges are distributed uniformly we can have three different types of charge density.
  1. Linear charge density, \(\lambda\text{:}\) If the charges are distributed uniformly in a linear shape then the charge density is called a linear charge density. It is defined as the amount of charge per unit length. Let say the charge Q be distributed smoothly on a wire of length L, then
    \begin{equation*} \lambda = \frac{Q}{L} \end{equation*}
    It’s unit is \(C/m.\) Now if a small length \(\,dl\) contains a charge \(\,dq \) then
    \begin{equation*} \,dq = \lambda \,dl \end{equation*}
  2. Surface charge density, \(\sigma:\) If the charges are distributed uniformly on a surface area then the charge density is called a surface charge density. It is defined as the amount of charge per unit area. Let say the charge Q be distributed smoothly on a plane of area A, then
    \begin{equation*} \sigma = \frac{Q}{A} \end{equation*}
    It’s unit is \(C/m^{2}.\) Now if a small area of that surface \(\,da\) contains a charge \(\,dq \) then
    \begin{equation*} \,dq = \sigma \,da \end{equation*}
  3. Volume charge density, \(\rho:\) If the charges are distributed uniformly in a volume of any material then the charge density is called a volume charge density. It is defined as the amount of charge per unit volume. Let say the charge Q bw distributed smoothly in a volume V, then
    \begin{equation*} \rho = \frac{Q}{V} \end{equation*}
    It’s unit is \(C/m^{3}.\) Now if a small volume of that material \(\,dv\) contains a charge \(\,dq \) then
    \begin{equation*} \,dq = \rho \,dv \end{equation*}
