  1. When two convex lenses are in contact:
    For lens \(C_{1}\text{,}\) we have-
    \begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{1}} =\frac{1}{s} +\frac{1}{s_{1}} \end{equation*}
    For lens \(C_{2}\text{,}\) we have-
    \begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{2}} =\frac{1}{-s_{1}} +\frac{1}{s_{2}} \end{equation*}
    Since image \(I_{1}\) behaves as a virtual object for lens \(C_{2}\text{.}\)
    Adding both terms we get -
    \begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{1}} +\frac{1}{f_{2}}=\frac{1}{s} +\frac{1}{s_{2}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{1}{F} = \frac{1}{f_{1}} +\frac{1}{f_{2}} \end{equation*}
    A reciprocal of equivalent focal length is a sum of the reciprocal of individual focal lengths of thin lenses being in contact.
  2. When two convex lenses are separated by a distance d apart:
    If a monochromatic light ray parallel to the common axis be incident on a lens \(C_{1}\) at height \(h_{1}\text{,}\) then the deviation produced by the first lens is given by
    \begin{equation*} \delta_{1} = \frac{h_{1}}{f_{1}}. \end{equation*}
    The emergent ray from lens \(C_{1}\) is refracted from lens \(C_{2}\) at height \(h_{2}\) and meets the axis at point F. Since the incident ray OA is parallel to the principle axis and after refraction through the combination meets the axis at F, hence F must be the principle focus of the combination. If
    \begin{equation*} \delta_{2} = \frac{h_{2}}{f_{2}} \end{equation*}
    is a deviation produced by the second lens \(C_{2}\text{,}\) then the total deviation produced by the combination is given by
    \begin{equation*} \delta = \delta_{1}+\delta_{2} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation} \Rightarrow\quad \frac{h_{1}}{f} = \frac{h_{1}}{f_{1}}+\frac{h_{2}}{f_{2}} \tag{5.2.33} \end{equation}
    From similar \(\triangle^{s} AC_{1}F_{1}\) and \(BC_{2}F_{1}\text{,}\) we have -
    \begin{equation*} \frac{BC_{2}}{AC_{1}} = \frac{C_{2}F_{1}}{C_{1}F_{1}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{h_{2}}{h_{1}} = \frac{f_{1}-d}{f_{1}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \Rightarrow \quad h_{2} = \frac{h_{1}(f_{1}-d)}{f_{1}} \end{equation*}
    Substituting the value of \(h_{2}\) in eqn. (5.2.33), we get -
    \begin{equation*} \frac{h_{1}}{f} = \frac{h_{1}}{f_{1}}+\frac{h_{1}(f_{1}-d)}{f_{1}f_{2}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{f_{1}}+\frac{1}{f_{2}}-\frac{d}{f_{1}f_{2}} \end{equation*}
