Item 1.

Open Pipe: An organ pipe which is open at its both ends is an open organ pipe. Flute, Trumpet, etc. are open organ pipes. Consider an open pipe of length, L whose both ends are open. When vibration is set at one end it reflects from the other end and produces standing waves of fundamental frequency \(f_{o}\) as shown in FigureÂ 4.5.2.
\begin{equation*} \text{Now}\quad L=\frac{\lambda_{1}}{4}+\frac{\lambda_{1}}{4}= \frac{\lambda_{1}}{2} \qquad \Rightarrow \lambda_{1} = 2L \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad f_{1} =\frac{v}{\lambda_{1}}= \frac{v}{2L}=f_{o} \quad \text{I Harmonics or fundamental frequency.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{again}\quad L=\frac{\lambda_{2}}{4} +\frac{\lambda_{2}}{2}+\frac{\lambda_{2}}{4} = \lambda_{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow \lambda_{2} = L \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and} \quad f_{2} = \frac{v}{\lambda_{2}} =\frac{v}{L} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad f_{2} = \frac{v}{L}=2\left(\frac{v}{2L}\right)=2f_{o} \quad \text{II Harmonics or I overtone.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{similarly}\quad L=\frac{\lambda_{3}}{2} +\frac{\lambda_{3}}{2}+\frac{\lambda_{3}}{2} = 3\frac{\lambda_{3}}{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow \lambda_{3} = \frac{2L}{3} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and} \quad f_{3} = \frac{v}{\lambda_{3}} =3\left(\frac{v}{2L}\right)=3f_{o} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad f_{3} = 3f_{o} \quad \text{III Harmonics or II overtone.} \end{equation*}
Hence the normal modes of vibration of an open pipe is given by
\begin{equation*} f_{n}=nf_{o}\qquad \text{where}\quad n=1,2,3,\cdots. \end{equation*}
