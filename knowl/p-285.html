In 1827, George Simon Ohm studied that the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference applied between its two ends at constant physical conditions (Temperature, light, electric, or magnetic field.) i.e.
\begin{equation*} I \propto V \quad \text{at constant T.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,} \quad I = k V\tag{2.1.2} \end{equation}
where \(k\) is a proportionality constant and known as a conductance of a conductor. Usually \(k = 1/R\text{,}\) \(R\) being resistance of the conductor. Hence
\begin{equation*} I=\frac{V}{R} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,} \quad V =IR \tag{2.1.3} \end{equation}
