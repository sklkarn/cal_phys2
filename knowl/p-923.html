If the angle of a prism is less than \(10^{o}\) then the prism is called a thin prism. In thin prism, A is very small and for small i, r, and e are also very small, hence we have -
\begin{equation*} {}^a\eta_{g} = \frac{i}{r}= \frac{\frac{A+\delta}{2}}{\frac{A}{2}} \end{equation*}
[\(\because \sin\theta\approx\theta\) for small \(\theta\)]
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \delta = A(\eta_{g}-1) \tag{5.2.22} \end{equation}
where \({}^a\eta_{g} = \eta_{g}\text{,}\) the absolute refractive index of glass.
