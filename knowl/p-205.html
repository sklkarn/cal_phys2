This is an outward mechanical force acting on a small elementary area \(\,dA.\) If this small element is displayed normally by a distance \(\,dy\) against this force then work done is given by
\begin{equation*} \,dW=F\,dy=\frac{1}{2}\epsilon_{o}E^{2}\,dA\,dy =\frac{1}{2}\epsilon_{o}E^{2} \,dV \end{equation*}
Therefore energy per unit volume is
\begin{equation*} \frac{\,dW}{\,dV}=\frac{\,dU}{\,dV}=\frac{1}{2}\epsilon_{o}E^{2} (J/m^{3}) \end{equation*}
This work done is stored in an electric field in the form of electric potential energy, i.e., the energy per unit volume of the medium or volume density of electrical energy in the region of an electric field is
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad u = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_{o} E^{2} \tag{1.7.14} \end{equation}
