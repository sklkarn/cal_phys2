For bright fringes,
\begin{equation*} \Delta x = \frac{yd}{D} = n\lambda \quad \text{where}\quad n=0,1,2,3,\cdots. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad y = \frac{n \lambda D}{d} \end{equation*}
At point O, path difference is zero and a bright fringe is formed, when
\begin{equation*} n=1, \quad y_{1} = \frac{\lambda D}{d} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} n=2, \quad y_{2} = \frac{2\lambda D}{d} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} n=3,\quad y_{3} = \frac{3\lambda D}{d} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y_{n} = \frac{n\lambda D}{d} \end{equation*}
Therefore the distance between any two consecutive bright fringes
\begin{equation*} y_{2}-y_{1} = \beta= \frac{2\lambda D}{d}-\frac{\lambda D}{d} = \frac{\lambda D}{d} \end{equation*}
