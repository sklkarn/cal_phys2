Magnetic field, \(\,dB\) due a small current element of length, \(\,dl\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \vec{dB} =\frac{\mu_{o}}{4\pi}\frac{I\vec{dl}\times\hat{r}}{r^{2}} \quad (Differential) \end{equation*}
where \(\hat{r}\) is a unit vector along the direction of \(\vec{r}.\) This law only works for a small current element. To find the magnetic field at a point due to entire current carrying conductor, we need to integrate the the above equation for the total length of the conductor.
\begin{equation*} \vec{B} =\frac{\mu_{o}}{4\pi}\int\frac{I\vec{dl}\times\hat{r}}{r^{2}} \quad (Integral) \end{equation*}
