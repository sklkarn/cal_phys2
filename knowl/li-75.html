Item iii.

Electric field at the point \(P_{3}\) [Figure 1.4.6] inside the cylindrical charge distribution i.e., \(r \lt R:\)
\begin{equation*} \phi = \oint\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,ds}= \oint E_{i}\,ds = E_{i}(2\pi r l) = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_{o}} = \frac{\rho \pi r^{2} l}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E_{i} = \frac{\rho r}{2 \epsilon_{o} } = \frac{\lambda r}{2 \pi \epsilon_{o} R^{2}} \end{equation*}
Thus the electric field inside the cylindrical charge distribution is proportional to the distance of the point from the axis and is directed radially away from the axis. The variation of field with distance \(r\) from the axis of a cylindrical charge is shown in Figure 1.4.3.(c).
