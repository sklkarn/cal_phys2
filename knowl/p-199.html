A parallel plate capacitor [FigureÂ 1.7.7] consists of two metal plates of large surface area placed close to each other parallely with one of the plates grounded. Let \(A\) be the surface area of the plates and they are separated by a distance \(d\) apart. If surface charge density of the plates are \(\sigma\) and space between the plates is filled with dielectric of permittivity \(\epsilon_{o}.\) Then the electric field between the plates is given by
\begin{equation*} E=E_{1}+E_{2}= \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{where,}\quad E_{1}= \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad E_{2}= \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
pointed along -ve plate.
\begin{equation*} \text{But,}\quad V = -\int\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,dr} = E\,d =\frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_{o}}d \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad \sigma = \frac{Q}{A} \end{equation*}
where \(V\) is the potential difference between the plates and the angle between \(\vec{\,dr}\) and \(\vec{E}\) is \(180^{o}.\)
\begin{equation*} C_{o} = \frac{Q}{V}= \frac{\sigma A}{Ed} = \frac{\sigma A}{\frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_{o}}d}=\frac{A \epsilon_{o}}{d} \end{equation*}
If the space between the plates is filled with dielectric with permitivity \(\epsilon\) then capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor is given by
\begin{equation} C=\frac{A \epsilon}{d}\tag{1.7.8} \end{equation}
