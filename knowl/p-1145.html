When objects size (such as plane mirror or curvature) are larger than the wavelength of light, then the behavior of light can be understood using ray optics such as in reflection, refraction, dispersion, etc. in geometric optics. On the other hand, when size of the objects are comparable to the wavelength of light, then the behavior of light when it interacts with the objects can only be understood using wave properties of light. Physical optics is a part of optics which deals with the phenomenon of light on the basis of wave nature of light such as interference, diffraction, polarization, etc.
