The direction of \(n^{th}\) order primary maximum for wavelength \(\lambda\) is given by
\begin{equation} (a+b)\sin\theta_{n}=n\lambda \tag{5.8.29} \end{equation}
The direction of \(n^{th}\) order primary maximum for wavelength \((\lambda+\,d\lambda)\) is given by
\begin{equation} (a+b)\sin(\theta_{n}+\,d\theta) = n(\lambda+\,d\lambda) \tag{5.8.30} \end{equation}
The two lines will be just resolved if the angle of diffraction \((\theta_{n}+\,d\theta)\) corresponds to the direction of first secondary minimum after the \(n^{th}\) primary maximum at \(P_{1}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad (a+b)\sin(\theta_{n}+\,d\theta) = n\lambda +\frac{\lambda}{N} \tag{5.8.31} \end{equation}
The term \(\frac{\lambda}{N}\) is the path difference of extreme points of first slit if there are N number of lines (slits) on the grating surface. Wavelength \(\lambda\) is the path difference between secondary waves from the first point of first slit and the last point of the last slit. Equating eqns. (5.8.30) and (5.8.31), we get -
\begin{equation*} n(\lambda+\,d\lambda) = n\lambda +\frac{\lambda}{N} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \frac{\lambda}{\,d\lambda} =nN =R \tag{5.8.32} \end{equation}
