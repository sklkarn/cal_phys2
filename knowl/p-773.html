As source of sound moves there is a change in wavelength perceived by the listener and if listener moves there is a change relative velocity of the sound can be measured by the listener. Hence the frequency (or pitch, \(f_{l}\)) of sound perceived by the listner can be determined by
\begin{equation*} f_{l} = \frac{v'}{\lambda'} \end{equation*}
where \(v'\) and \(\lambda'\) are the relative velocity of sound and effective wavelength of sound wave for the listner.
