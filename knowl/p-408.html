Consider a differential charge, \(\,dq\) to be moving with a velocity, \(\vec{v} \) in a magnetic field, \(\vec{B}\) then differential force acting on a charge is given by
\begin{equation*} \vec{\,dF} = \,dq (\vec{v}\times\vec{B}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad\vec{\,dF} = \rho\,dV (\vec{v}\times\vec{B}) = (\rho\vec{v}\times\vec{B})\,dV \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = (\vec{J}\times\vec{B})\,dV= (\vec{J}\,dV\times\vec{B}) = I\vec{\,dl}\times\vec{B} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \vec{F}= I\vec{l}\times\vec{B}\tag{3.2.1} \end{equation}
Here, \(\rho, \,dV, \,l, \,\vec{J}, \,I \) are volume charge density, volume, length, current density, and current of a conductor.
