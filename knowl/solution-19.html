Solution 1.5.4.1.

Given: \(a=0.2\,m; \quad b=2\,N/C; \quad c=10 \,N/Cm;\) \(\epsilon_{o}=8.85\times 10^{-12}\,C^{2}/(Nm^{2})\)
\begin{equation*} \vec{E_{x}} = (b+cx)\hat{i}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Phi_{E}=\int \vec{E} \cdot \vec{dA} =\frac{q}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} A_{1} = a^{2} = A_{2}=A_{3}=A_{4}=A_{5}=A_{6} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} x_{1}=a/2; \quad x_{2}=-a/2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Phi_{1}=E_{x_{1}}A_{1}=0.12 \,N m^{2}/C; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Phi_{2}=-E_{x_{2}}A_{2}=-0.04 \,N m^{2}/C; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Phi_{3}=\Phi_{4}=\Phi_{5}=\Phi_{6}=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Phi_{E} = \Phi_{1}+\Phi_{2}+\Phi_{3}+\Phi_{4}+\Phi_{5}+\Phi_{6} = 0.08 \,Nm^{2}/C \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad q=\epsilon_{o}\Phi_{E} =7.08\times10^{-13}\,C \end{equation*}
