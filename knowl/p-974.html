The condition of removing chromatic aberration from a lens is called an achromatism. When two lenses a convex lens and a concave lens of different materials are combined in such a way that the combination will not produce any chromatic aberration then the lens is called an achromatic lens. Suppose a crown glass convex lens and a flint glass concave lens are combined together, then from lens formula
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f} = (\eta -1)\left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}}\right)=\frac{1}{(\eta -1)f}=K (say) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{1}{f}= K(\eta-1) \end{equation*}
Differentiating, we get -
\begin{equation*} -\frac{\,df}{f^{2}}=K\,d\eta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{\,d\eta}{f(\eta-1)}=\frac{\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}}{f(\eta-1)} = \frac{\omega}{f} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \omega=-\frac{\,df}{f} \end{equation*}
That is the dispersive power can be defined as the relative change in the focal length of a lens and the -ve sign indicates that the focal length of the lens decreases with increasing refractive index of the material. If \(f_{1}\) and \(f_{2}\) are the focal lengths of the two lenses of different materials (crown and flint) placed in contact coaxially, then the equivalent focal length of the combination is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{F}=\frac{1}{f_{1}}+\frac{1}{f_{2}} \end{equation*}
Differentiating we get -
\begin{equation*} -\frac{\,dF}{F^{2}}= -\frac{\,df_{1}}{f_{1}^{2}}-\frac{\,df_{2}}{f_{2}^{2}} =\frac{\omega}{f_{1}} +\frac{\omega'}{f_{2}} \end{equation*}
For the combination to be achromatic, \(-\frac{\,dF}{F^{2}}\) must be equal to zero. Hence
\begin{equation*} \frac{\omega}{f_{1}} +\frac{\omega'}{f_{2}} = 0 \end{equation*}
which is the condition for achromatism.
