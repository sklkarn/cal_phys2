The beam of light incident parallel to the principal axis will converge when passing through a convex lens, and diverge when passing through a concave lens. Since the convex lens converges the incident light rays it is also known as converging lens and the concave lens diverges the incident light rays it is also known as diverging lens (Figure 5.2.34.(a) and Figure 5.2.34.(b)). In a lens it is convenient to take 2F points on the principle axis on both sides of the lens to draw ray diagram. It is just double the focal length of the lens but not the center of curvature.
