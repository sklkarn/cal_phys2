Solution 1.3.4.1.

Given: \(q_{1} = 1.00 \times 10^{-6} \,C; \quad q_{2} = -2.00 \times 10^{-6} \,C;\) \(q_{3} = 3.00 \times10^{-6} \,C; \) \(k = 9.00 \times 10^{9} \,N m^{2}/C^{2};\) \(r_{12} = 5 \times 10^{-2} \,m.\)
  1. Let the charges are located in a line as shown in figure below, then magnitude of force on \(3^{rd}\) charge due to \(1^{st}\) and \(2^{nd}\) charges is given by
    \begin{equation*} |\vec{F}_{31}| = k\frac{q_{1}q_{3}}{r_{13}^{2}}; \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} |\vec{F}_{32}| = k\frac{q_{2}q_{3}}{r_{23}^{2}} \end{equation*}
    Since there is not net force acting on \(3^{rd} \) charge, [figure (a)] hence
    \begin{equation*} \vec{F}=\vec{F}_{31} + \vec{F}_{32} = 0 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad k\frac{q_{1}q_{3}}{r_{13}^{2}} + k\frac{q_{2}q_{3}}{r_{23}^{2}} =0 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{q_{1}}{r_{13}^{2}} = - \frac{q_{2}}{r_{23}^{2}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{ 1.00 \times 10^{-6}}{x^{2}} = \frac{ 2.00 \times 10^{-6} }{(r_{12}+ x)^{2}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{ 1}{x^{2}} = \frac{ 2}{(5 + x)^{2}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad 2x^{2} = x^{2}+10x+25 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad x^{2} - 10x-25 =0 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad x = \frac{10 \pm \sqrt{100+4\cdot1\cdot25}}{2\cdot1} = 12.1 \,cm, -2.1 \,cm \end{equation*}
    Here we have taken x = -2.1 cm to obtain no net force on the \(3^{rd}\) charge. Hence x=\(r_{13}\)=-2.1 cm = -0.02 m.
  2. \begin{equation*} \vec{F}_{1} = \vec{F}_{12} + \vec{F}_{13} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} = k\frac{q_{1}q_{2}}{r^{2}_{12}} (\hat{i})+k\frac{q_{1}q_{3}}{r^{2}_{13}} (\hat{i}) \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} = 9\times 10^{-3}(800 + 7500)(\hat{i}) = 74.7 (\hat{i}) N \end{equation*}
    here, \(\hat{i}\) is a unit vector along x-axis [figure (b)]. Here only magnitude of charges are taken in calculation as the direction of forces are already determined according to nature of charges.
  3. \begin{equation*} \vec{F}_{2} = \vec{F}_{21} + \vec{F}_{23} = 18.2 (-\hat{i}) N \end{equation*}
