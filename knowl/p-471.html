The force experienced by the conductor of current \(I_{2}\) is
\begin{equation*} F_{2}= I_{2}LB_{1} = I_{2}L \frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}}{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{F_{2}}{L} = \frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}}{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
The force experienced by the conductor of current \(I_{1}\) is
\begin{equation*} \frac{F_{1}}{L} = \frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}}{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{F}{L}=\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}}{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
The direction of forces are shown in Figure 3.5.6.(a). Since the two forces are pointed along each other meaning the conductors attract each other. Hence two current carrying conductors attract each other if currents are flowing parallel to each other. If currents are flowing anti-parallel to each other then they repel each other as shown in Figure 3.5.6.(b).
