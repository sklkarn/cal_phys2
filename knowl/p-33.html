Electric Dipole: It is a small displacement between two equal and opposite charges. It acquires rotational effect if exposed in an external field and hence it is also known as electric dipole moment, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \vec{p} = q d (\hat{u}), \end{equation*}
where d is a distance between two equal charges and \(\hat{u}\) is unit vector along the line joining from -ve charge to +ve charge. Dipole moment is a vector quantity whose direction is pointed along negative to positive charge. When a dipole is placed in an external electric field it shows a rotating effect.
