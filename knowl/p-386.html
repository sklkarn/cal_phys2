A bar magnet can be considered as composed of many tiny current loops. The currents on the loop around the periphery of a bar magnet at each cross-sectional plane survive producing a resultant current around the outer surface whereas inner currents cancel out one another within the cross-sectional plane. Hence the cause of any magnet is intrinsic current loop around the outer surface of a magnet.
