Let MN be the surface separating two media a and b having refracting indices of \(\eta_{1}\) and \(\eta_{2}\text{,}\)respectively as shown in FigureÂ 5.2.20. Let velocity of light in medium a is \(v_{a}\) and medium b is \(v_{b}\) where \(v_{a} \gt v_{b}\text{.}\) Consider a plane wavefront APB is coming to incident on plane MN. By the time the disturbance at B reaches to C, the secondary waves from A must have traveled a distance \(AD=v_{b}t\) where t is the time taken by the waves to travel the distance BC.
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad BC=v_{a}t \qquad \text{and} \quad AD =v_{b}t \end{equation*}
As velocity of light is less in medium b, the refracted wavefront is given by the tangent CD drawn at point D, where AD is a radius of sphere centered at point A. The line CD happens to be an envelop of all the secondary wavelets of wavefront APB coming from medium a to medium b.
