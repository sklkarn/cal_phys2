    Section 3.5 Amper‚Äôs Law

    Ampere‚Äôs Law states that the line integral of the tangential component of a magnetic field B around a closed path is proportional to the current enclosed \(I_{enc}\) by the loop. i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} \oint\vec{B}\cdot\vec{dl} = \mu_{o} I_{enc} \end{equation*}
    Right Hand Rule.

    Proof.

    The magnetic field at a point P on the circular path due to current carrying wire is given by
    \begin{equation*} B=\frac{\mu_{o}I}{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
    Magnetic field is parallel to the tangential direction at any point on circular path. Hence line integral of the field around the circular path centered on the current carrying wire is given by
    \begin{equation*} \oint\vec{B}\cdot\vec{\,dl} = \oint B\,dl\cos\theta = \oint B \,dl \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} =\oint \frac{\mu_{o}I}{2\pi r} (r\,d\theta)= \frac{\mu_{o}I}{2\pi} (2\pi)=\mu_{o}I \end{equation*}
    Thus we have -
    \begin{equation*} \oint\vec{B}\cdot\vec{dl} = \mu_{o} I_{enc} \end{equation*}
    The sign of integral is +ve if path follwed for line integral is parallel to B and -ve if the path followed is anti-parallel. Ampere‚Äôs law holds for closed path of any shape.
    Ampere‚Äôs law is an anlogous to Gauss‚Äôs law in electrostatics and is useful to determine magnetic field when the current distribution is symmetrical. Amperean equation always holds good whether the current distribution is symmetrical or not but we can only use the equation to determine B when symmetrical current distribution exists. Ampere‚Äôs law is a special case of Biot-Savart‚Äôs law. In order to apply Ampere‚Äôs Law.
    1. Current must be steady (ie., current is not changing with time).
    2. Only currents enclosed by the loop are taken into account.
    3. Currents have to be taken with their algebraic signs using right hand‚Äôs rule to determine directions and signs (currents going out of the surface are positive and those going in are negative).
