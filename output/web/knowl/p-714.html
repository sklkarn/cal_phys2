Youngâ€™s Modulus, \(Y\text{:}\) It is the ratio of longitudinal stress to the longitudinal strain, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} Y=\frac{F/A}{\Delta L/L}, \end{equation*}
where F is applied force normal to the cross-section of a wire, A is cross-sectional area of a wire, L is length of the wire, and \(\Delta L\) is change in length of the wire after the application of this force. It describes the ability of a material to deform its length under the lengthwise stress. Its unit is Pascal (Pa).
