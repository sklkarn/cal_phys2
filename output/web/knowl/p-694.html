Group Velocity: The velocity at which energy is transported is called a group velocity. In reality, any signal consists of packets of waves of different frequencies, which travel together. The speed of the wave packets is called the group velocity. The group velocity [FigureÂ 4.2.4] is a bit slower than the phase velocity of the individual waves. The packet moves slower than the single-frequency wave. If the medium is dispersionless then the group and phase velocity are the same.
