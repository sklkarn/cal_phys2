For dark fringes
\begin{equation*} \Delta x = \frac{yd}{D} = (2n+1)\frac{\lambda}{2} \quad \text{where}\quad n=0,1,2,3,\cdots. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad y = \frac{(2n+1) \lambda D}{2d} \end{equation*}
For a dark fringe, when
\begin{equation*} n=0, \quad y_{0} = \frac{\lambda D}{2d} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} n=1, \quad y_{1} = \frac{3\lambda D}{2d} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} n=2,\quad y_{2} = \frac{5\lambda D}{2d} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y_{n} = \frac{(2n+1)\lambda D}{2d} \end{equation*}
Therefore the distance between any two consecutive dark fringes
\begin{equation*} y_{2}-y_{1} = \beta= \frac{5\lambda D}{2d}-\frac{3\lambda D}{2d} = \frac{\lambda D}{d} \end{equation*}
The fringe width is same for bright or dark fringe.
