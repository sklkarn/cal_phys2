Thus the electric field at any point close to the surface of a charged conductor of any shape is equal to \(\frac{1}{\epsilon_{o}}\) times the surface charge density \(\sigma\text{.}\) If \(\sigma\) is positive, electric field is directed away from the conductor and if it is negative, then field is directed towards the conductor. Obviously field near a plane conductor is twice of the field in front of a non-conducting thin sheet of charge.
