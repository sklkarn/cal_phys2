Four electrical charges are located at the corners of a square. The first charge is \(+10 \,\mu C \) at (0,0), the second is \(-20 \,\mu C\) at (1m,0), the third is \(+30 \,\mu C\) at (1 m, 1 m), and the fourth is \(-40 \,\mu C\) at (0, 1 m).
  1. What is the magnitude and direction of the electrical force on the \(10 \,\mu C\) charge?
  2. If gravity acts in the -y-direction and the first charge has a mass of 100 g, what is the magnitude and direction of the initial acceleration of the first mass if released from rest and allowed to move under the influence of gravitational and electrical forces.
