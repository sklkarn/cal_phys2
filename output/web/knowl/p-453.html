\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad B=\frac{\mu_{o}nI}{2}\int\limits_{\alpha}^{\beta}\sin\phi\,d\phi \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{\mu_{o}nI}{2}\left(-\cos\phi\right)_{\alpha}^{\beta} =\frac{\mu_{o}nI}{2}\left(\cos\alpha-\cos\beta\right) \end{equation*}
For an infinitely long solenoid, \(\alpha\rightarrow 0, \quad \beta\rightarrow \pi. \) Hence,
\begin{equation*} B=\frac{\mu_{o}nI}{2}\left(1+1\right) = \mu_{o}nI \end{equation*}
If point P is at the end of a solenoid, \(\alpha\rightarrow \frac{\pi}{2}, \quad \beta\rightarrow \pi.\) Hence,
\begin{equation*} B=\frac{\mu_{o}nI}{2} \end{equation*}
Thus for a long solenoid the magnetic field at the ends is half of that at the middle of the solenoid.
