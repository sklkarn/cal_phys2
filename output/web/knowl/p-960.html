The refractive index of the material for a prism is different for different wavelengths (or colors) which is given by Cauchy’s dispersion formula
\begin{equation*} \eta = A+\frac{B}{\lambda^{2}}. \end{equation*}
The values of A and B depend on the medium and is called Cauchy’s constants. Since
\begin{equation*} \eta \propto \frac{1}{\lambda^{2}} \end{equation*}
the violet light suffers the greatest deviation and red light suffers the least deviation in visible light spectrum. The color of light is dependent on its wavelength, \(\lambda\text{.}\) Here is a list of wavelengths
\begin{equation*} \lambda_{red}=620-750\,nm, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lambda_{orange}=590-620\,nm, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lambda_{yellow}=570-620\,nm, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lambda_{green}=495-620\,nm, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lambda_{blue}=450-495\,nm, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lambda_{red}=380-450\,nm. \end{equation*}
