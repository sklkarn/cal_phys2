Solution 3.8.3.1.

The area of a circle can be determined by
\begin{equation*} \,dA = \text{area of shaded region} = \frac{1}{2}r\,ds \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{1}{2}r (r\,d\theta) =\frac{1}{2}r^{2} \,d\theta \end{equation*}
Therefore change in magnetic flux,
\begin{equation*} \varepsilon = emf= -\frac{\,d\Phi}{\,dt} = -B\left(\frac{-\,dA}{\,dt}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{1}{2}r^{2}B\left(\frac{\,d\theta}{\,dt}\right) = \frac{1}{2}r^{2}B \omega \end{equation*}
Current induced in the loop is
\begin{equation*} I=\frac{emf}{R} \end{equation*}
The direction of current is clockwise as the loop opposes the flux to be decreased hence current is induced in a circuit to substitute the decreasing flux. Use Flemingâ€™s right hand rule to determine the direction of induced current.
