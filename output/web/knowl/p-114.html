The resultant vector \(\vec{r'}\) or the subtraction of \(\vec{b}\) from \(\vec{a}\) is the addition of a negative vector of \(\vec{b}\) (i.e., \(-\vec{b}\)) to a \(\vec{a}\text{,}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \vec{r'} = \vec{a} - \vec{b} = \vec{a} + (-\vec{b}) \end{equation*}
as shown in FigureÂ 2.1.1.(d). The resultant vector can be obtained by arranging the two individual vectors in such a way that the head of one vector falls onto the tail of another one without changing their individual directions. The resultant vector then joins the tail of first vector to the head of second vector (Remember the resultant vector is not in cyclic orientation of the individual vectors). If a vector \(\vec{a}\) is multiplied by any real positive number \(n\text{,}\) then the product \(n \vec{a}\) is a vector whose magnitude is \(n\) times as that of \(\vec{a}\) and its direction is the same as \(\vec{a}\text{.}\) If \(n\) is a negative number then the direction of \(n \vec{a}\) is opposite to \(\vec{a}\text{.}\) If \(\hat{a}\) is a unit vector in the direction of \(\vec{a}\) then, we have
  1. \(\vec{a} = |a| \hat{a},\) where \(|a|\) is a magnitude of \(\vec{a}\text{.}\)
  2. \((k+l)\vec{a} = (k+l)a \hat{a} = k a \hat{a} + l a \hat{a} = k \vec{a} + l \vec{a},\) where \(k\) and \(l\) are scalars.
  3. \(k(\vec{a} + \vec{b}) = k \vec{a} + k\vec{b}\text{.}\)
  4. \(k(l\vec{a}) = (k l) \vec{a}\text{.}\)
