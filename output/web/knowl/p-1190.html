If the width of slit is very narrow such as \(a \lt \lt \lambda\text{,}\) then in the double-slit experiment, the central maximum of the diffraction pattern of either slit covers the entire viewing screen and the interference of light produces bright fringes with approximately the same intensity. In practice, however, the condition \(a \lt \lt \lambda\) is not met. For relatively wide slits FigureÂ 5.8.12.(a), the interference of light from two slits produces bright fringes that do not all have the same intensity. That is, the intensities of the fringes produced by double-slit interference are modified by diffraction of the light passing through each slit.
