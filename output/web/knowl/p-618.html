Suppose
\begin{equation*} P=IV \end{equation*}
is power generated at the power stations. During transmitting this power to the destination
\begin{equation*} P' = I^{2}R = IV' \end{equation*}
power is lost as heat disspation in the transmission line. To reduce this loss \(P'\) (or, \(V' = IR\)) should be made as small as possible which can be obtain by the follwing two ways. Since
\begin{equation*} R=\frac{\rho L}{A} \end{equation*}
where \(\rho\) is a resistivity of the material of transmission line, L is its length, and A is its area of cross-section. Hence to make R small wire should be made thicker (or A is high) but in this case transmission line becomes heavier and costly. Another way is to make V as high as possible and I as small as possible so that
\begin{equation*} P=IV \end{equation*}
remains the same and power loss
\begin{equation*} P' = I^{2}R \end{equation*}
is reduced significantly. For example, suppose V is made 1000 times higher and I is 1000 times smaller then power loss
\begin{equation*} P" = \left(\frac{I}{1000}\right)^{2}R = 10^{-6}I^{2}R =10^{-6} P" \end{equation*}
i.e., new power loss \(P"\) is a million times smaller than power loss of \(P'\text{.}\) To convert voltage or current from high to low or from low to high is only possible with the help of transformer which can only be operated in alternating current under the electromagnetic induction. Hence ac is preferential over dc.
