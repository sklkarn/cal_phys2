A spherical capacitor consists of two concentric conducting spherical shells [Figure 1.7.8] of inner radius \(a\) and outer radius \(b\) such that \(b \gt a\) and space between them is filled with dielectric of permittivity \(\epsilon_{o}\text{.}\) If a charge \(+Q\) is given to the inner shell then \(-Q\) amount of charge is induced on the inner surface of outer shell and charge \(+Q\) on the outer surface of outer shell. As the outer surface is grounded charge \(+Q\) neutralizes with the negative charge coming from the earth. Now using Gauss’s law the electric field at any point between the shells is given
