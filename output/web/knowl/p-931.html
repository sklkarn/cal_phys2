Paragraph

A lens can be thought of as made up of a number of truncated prisms of varying refracting angle pile on top of each other on both sides. The refracting angle of a prism at the middle portion is infinite hence it behaves like a simple glass slab. The refracting angles of the prisms above or below the middle section of the lens gradually increase positively for convex lens and negatively for concave lens as shown in Figure 5.2.35.(a) and Figure 5.2.35.(b). Since the prism bends the ray of light towards its base light converges in convex lens and diverges in concave lens after passing through the lens.
