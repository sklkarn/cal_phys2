The magnetic field at point P’ inside the conductor [Figure 3.5.2.(b)] can be determined by considering a finite thickness of current carrying rod of radius a and the point P’ is at distance b from the axis of conductor then current enclosed by the Amperean loop passing through P’ is \(I' \lt I.\) If \(J=\frac{I}{\pi r^{2}}\) is a current density then,
\begin{equation*} I' = J\cdot\pi b^{2} = \frac{I}{\pi r^{2}}\cdot\pi b^{2}= I\frac{b^{2}}{a^{2}}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad B' = \frac{\mu_{o}I'}{2\pi b} =\frac{\mu_{o}I b^{2}}{2\pi b a^{2}} = \frac{\mu_{o}I b}{2\pi a^{2}} \end{equation*}
Magnetic field is directly proportional to the distance of the point within the current carrying conductor [Figure 3.5.2.(c)].
