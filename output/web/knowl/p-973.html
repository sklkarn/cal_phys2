In greek chroma means color and aberration means defect, hence the color defects of a lens is called a chromatic aberration. In other words, the inability of a lens to focus all colors of white light at a single focus is known as chromatic aberration. From lens maker’s formula it is clear that the focal length of a lens depend upon the refractive index of a material of lens which is the cause of chromatic aberration. If white light is passing through the lens violet color focuses at a point \(F_{v}\) near to the lens and the red color focuses at point \(F_{r}\) far from the lens. The rays of intermediate colors are focused between these points as shown in Figure 5.3.5. Hence the image of an object emitting white light stretched between \(F_{v}\) and \(F_{r}\) and is blurred. Chromatic aberration is of two types, axial (or longitudinal) aberration and lateral (or transverse) aberration.
