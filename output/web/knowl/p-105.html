Consider an infinitely long cylinder of radius R on which electric charge is distributed uniformly. If \(\lambda\) is charge per unit length and \(\rho\) is a volume charge density then
\begin{equation*} \rho (\pi R^{2}l)= \lambda l \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \rho = \frac{\lambda}{\pi R^{2}} \end{equation*}
