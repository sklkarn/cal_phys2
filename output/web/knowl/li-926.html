Item iii.

For a lens of \(R_{1}=R_{2} =R\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} f= \frac{R}{2(\eta -1)} \quad \text{for convex lens} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f= \frac{-R}{2(\eta -1)} \quad \text{for concave lens} \end{equation*}
For a glass lens of \(\eta =1.5\text{,}\) \(f_{convex}= +R\) and \(f_{concave}= -R\text{.}\)That is, for a symmetrical glass lens placed in air, focal length is equal to the radius of curvature of the lens.
