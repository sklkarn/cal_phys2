Orsted discovered that electric currents can produce magnetic fields. Faraday discovered that magnetic fields can be used to induce electric currents. It has been seen from experiments that whenever there is a change in magnetic flux linked with the closed circuit, am emf (or current) is induced in the circuit and lasts as long as this change happen. This phenomenon is known as electromagnetic induction. The magnetic flux through a circuit may be changed in the following ways:
  1. by relative motion between a magnet and the circuit,
  2. by changing current in the same circuit,
  3. by changing current in the neighbouring circuit, and
  4. by rotating a coil in a magnetic field.
