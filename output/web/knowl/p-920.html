From eqns. (5.2.17) and (5.2.18), we get -
\begin{equation} A=r_{1} +r_{2} \tag{5.2.19} \end{equation}
Now from eqns. (5.2.16) and (5.2.19), we get -
\begin{equation} \delta = (i+e)-A \tag{5.2.20} \end{equation}
But at minimum deviation condition, \(\delta_{min}\) we have -
\begin{equation*} i=e, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} and, \qquad r_{1}=r_{2}=r \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} Hence, \quad \delta=\delta_{min} = 2i-A \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow i=\frac{A+\delta_{min}}{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{also,} \quad r=\frac{A}{2} \end{equation*}
Now from (5.2.15), we have-
\begin{equation} {}^a\eta_{g} =\frac{\sin \frac{A+\delta_{min}}{2}}{\sin \frac{A}{2}}\tag{5.2.21} \end{equation}
This is the relation between refractive index and angle of minimum deviation.
