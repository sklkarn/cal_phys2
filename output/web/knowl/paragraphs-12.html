12.

A parallel-plate capacitor has a capacitance of 500 pF and a voltage of 100 V. If the separation between the plates is 0.5 mm, find
  1. the charge on the capacitor,
  2. area of the capacitor,
  3. the electric field between the plates,
  4. the energy stored in the capacitor,
  5. the charge density on the surface of the plates,
  6. the energy density of the electric field between the plates.

13.

  1. the spacing between the two spheres,
  2. the potential difference between the two spheres,
  3. the energy stored in the capacitor,
  4. the charge density on the inner sphere,
  5. the charge density on the outer sphere,
  6. the electric field mid-way between the spheres,
  7. the average energy density between the two spheres.

14.

  3. the energy stored in the capacitor,
  4. the electric field mid-way between the cylinders,
  5. the energy density of the electric field mid-way between the capacitors,

15.

  1. charge on the capacitor,
  2. the energy stored in the capacitor.
  3. If the charge on the capacitor is kept constant while the distance between the plates is doubled, what happens to the electric field between the plates?

  5. How much work must be done to separate the plates?
  6. What is the new capacitance when the separation is doubled?
  7. If the area of the plates is \(0.2 m^{2},\) what is the force between the two plates?
