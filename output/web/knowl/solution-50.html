Solution 3.6.4.1.

Pick a circle centered on the wire and perpendicular to the wire. Due to the right hand rule, the magnetic field must be tangent to the circle at every point. Therefore, the dot product
\begin{equation*} \vec{B}\cdot\vec{dl} = B dl. \end{equation*}
Due to symmetry, B must have the same magnitude at every point, so B is constant and Ampereâ€™s Law becomes:
\begin{equation*} \oint \vec{B}\cdot\vec{dl} = \mu_{o}I \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} B\oint\,dl = B(2\pi r) = \mu_{o}I \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} B=\frac{\mu_{o}I }{2\pi r} \end{equation*}
