If light wave is propagating along x-axis, then electric field \(\vec{E}\) oscillates in the xy plane and magnetic field \(\vec{B}\) oscillates in the xz plane. Normally, any light source emits unpolarized light, i.e. electric field could vibrates along all possible directions perpendicular to the light propagation. Hence, polarization is a phenomenon of restricting the vibration of electric filed in a single plane. Light is said to be linearly polarized if the direction of oscillation of electric field is confined to one direction. Polarization direction is always perpendicular to the direction of propagation and electric field vector oscillates in a plane of polarization.
