From Snell’s law
\begin{equation*} \eta_{1}\sin\theta_{1} = \eta_{2}\sin \theta_{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \eta_{1} i = \eta_{2} r \end{equation*}
\(\because \quad \theta\) is a very small. Hence, \(\sin\theta \simeq \theta \) and \(\theta_{1} =i, \quad \theta_{2}=r\) [Figure 5.2.29.(a)].
\begin{equation*} \text{but,}\quad i = \alpha+\beta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad \beta=r+\gamma \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{ or,} \quad \eta_{1} (\alpha+\beta) = \eta_{2} (\beta-\gamma) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{ or,} \quad \eta_{1} \alpha+ n_{2}\gamma = (\eta_{2}-\eta_{1}) \beta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{ or,} \quad \eta_{1} \frac{PM}{OM}+ \eta_{2}\frac{PM}{MI} = (\eta_{2}-\eta_{1})\frac{PM}{MC} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{ or,} \quad \frac{\eta_{1}}{s}+ \frac{\eta_{2}}{s'} = \frac{\eta_{2}-\eta_{1}}{R} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \frac{\eta}{s}+ \frac{\eta'}{s'}= \frac{\eta' -\eta}{R} \tag{5.2.23} \end{equation}
