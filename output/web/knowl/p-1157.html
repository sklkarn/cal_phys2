If \(y_{1}\) and \(y_{2}\) are the displacement of the particle at point P on the screen when the waves from \(S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\) reach at P, then the resultant displacement at P is given by the principle of superposition
\begin{equation*} y=y_{1}+y_{2} = a\sin(kx-\omega t)+ a\sin(kx-\omega t +\delta) \end{equation*}
where a is amplitude of the waves and \(\delta\) is their phase difference at P.
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad y = a\left[2\sin\left(\frac{kx-\omega t+kx-\omega t+\delta}{2}\right)\cos\left(\frac{kx-\omega t-kx+\omega t-\delta}{2}\right)\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad y = 2a\sin\left(kx-\omega t+\frac{\delta}{2}\right)\cos\left(\frac{\delta}{2}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad y = A sin\left(kx-\omega t +\frac{\delta}{2}\right) \end{equation*}
