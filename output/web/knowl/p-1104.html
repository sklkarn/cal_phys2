Again, energy flowing out of the sphere per unit area per second is given by
\begin{equation*} I_{1}=\frac{Q}{4\pi R_{1}^{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} I_{2}=\frac{Q}{4\pi R_{2}^{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \frac{I_{1}}{I_{2}}=\frac{R_{2}^{2}}{R_{1}^{2}} \tag{5.6.13} \end{equation}
From eqns. (5.6.12) and (5.6.13), we have -
\begin{equation*} \frac{I_{1}}{I_{2}}=\frac{S_{2}}{S_{1}}=\frac{R_{2}^{2}}{R_{1}^{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad I \propto \frac{1}{R^{2}} \tag{5.6.14} \end{equation}
This is a famous inverse square law which states that the amount of light energy falling on a given surface from a point source is inversely proportional to the square of the distance between the surface and the source.
