Item I.

I Law: It is also called a current law. This law is based on the principle of conservation of charge. It states that in an electrical circuit the algebraic sum of currents at a point is zero, i.e., the currents entering to a point is equal to currents going out of the point as shown in Figure 2.4.1.(a). Mathematically,
\begin{equation} \sum I=0\tag{2.4.1} \end{equation}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad I_{5}+I_{6}-I_{1}-I_{2}-I_{3}-I_{4}=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} i.e., \quad I_{1}+I_{2}+I_{3}+I_{4}=I_{5}+I_{6} \end{equation*}
Thus in an electrical circuit, there is no accumulation of charge at any point or junction.
Junction or Node: It is a point in a network where two or more than two circuit elements meet together as points \(A,\, B, \,P,\) or \(Q\) in Figure 2.4.1.(b). Points A, H, and D are the same point or the same junction. It is a shared point between electrical components. The voltage at any point on the junction is same.
Loop: It is a closed circuit along which a current can flow. The closed circuit ABCDA is one loop, ABGHA is another loop, and DCGHD is the other loop as shown in Figure 2.4.1.(b).
