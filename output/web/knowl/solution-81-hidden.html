Given: \(n=1, \quad n'=1.5,\quad R_{1} = -8\,cm,\quad R_{2} = -9\,cm.\) For first concave surface,
\begin{equation*} \frac{n}{s}+\frac{n'}{s'}=\frac{n'-n}{R_{1}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or,\quad \frac{1}{8}+\frac{1.5}{s'}=\frac{1.5-1}{-8} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow s'=-8 \,cm. \end{equation*}
The image of the object is formed at the center of first sphere and is virtual.
For second concave surface,
\begin{equation*} \frac{n}{s}+\frac{n'}{s'}=\frac{n'-n}{R_{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or,\quad \frac{1}{9}+\frac{1.5}{s'}=\frac{1.5-1}{-9} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \Rightarrow s'=-9 \,cm. \end{equation*}
Thus the final image is also formed at the center and is virtual.
