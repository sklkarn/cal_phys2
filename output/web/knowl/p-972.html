The axial chromatic aberration is given by
\begin{equation*} x=f_{r}-f_{b}. \end{equation*}
Now the focal length of a lens is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f}=(\eta-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}}\right) = \frac{1}{f(\eta-1)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{Similarly,}\quad \frac{1}{f_{b}}=(\eta'_{b}-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}}\right)= \frac{(\eta'_{b}-1)}{f(\eta-1)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{Also,}\quad \frac{1}{f_{r}}=(\eta'_{r}-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}-\frac{1}{R_{2}}\right)= \frac{(\eta'_{r}-1)}{f(\eta-1)} \end{equation*}
Hence
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{f_{b}} -\frac{1}{f_{r}} = \frac{(\eta_{b}-1)-(\eta_{r}-1)}{f(\eta-1)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{f_{r}-f_{b}}{f_{b}f_{r}} = \frac{\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}}{f(\eta-1)} \end{equation*}
Taking
\begin{equation*} f_{b}f_{r} =f^{2} \end{equation*}
as f is a geometrical mean of \(f_{b}\) and \(f_{r}\) and \(f\) being the focal length of mean light, we have -
\begin{equation*} f_{r}-f_{b} = \frac{(\eta_{b}-\eta_{r})f}{\eta-1}=\omega f \end{equation*}
Hence it is clear that a single lens can not produce an image free from chromatic aberration unless the dispersive power can be balanced by using some other material.
