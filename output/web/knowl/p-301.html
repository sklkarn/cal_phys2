To understand internal resistance and emf lets consider a lead-acid cell. However, the similar senario could be applied for any other alkaline cells. Now \(H^{+}\) ion migrates towards \(PbO_{2} \) electrode, captures electron from the electrode and becomes nacent hydrogen atom. Nacent \(H\) then reacts with \(PbO_{2}\) and forms \(H_{2}O\) and \(PbO\text{.}\) \(PbO\) instantly reacts with \(H_{2} SO_{4}\) molecule and converted into \(PbSO_{4}\text{.}\) As \(+ve\) charges start accumulating on \(PbO_{2} \) electrode, we call it a \(+ \) plate of the cell.
