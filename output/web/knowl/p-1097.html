\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \omega = \frac{S}{r^{2}} \tag{5.6.11} \end{equation}
where S is the surface area of the object perpendicular to the rays subtending the angle at the point and r is the distance of object from the point. If S is oblique to r then,
\begin{equation*} \omega= \frac{S\cos\theta}{r^{2}}. \end{equation*}
Its SI unit is steradian (st.rad.).
