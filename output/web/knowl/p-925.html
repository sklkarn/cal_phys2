If a small object of length \(\,ds\) is placed along the axis of a spherical medium, it produces an image of length \(\,ds'\) along the axis, hence the Longitudinal magnification,
\begin{equation*} L=\frac{\,ds'}{\,ds} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{but,}\quad \frac{\eta}{s}+ \frac{\eta'}{s'}= \frac{\eta' -\eta}{R} \end{equation*}
Differentiating w.r.t. ’s’, we get -
\begin{equation*} -\frac{\eta}{s^{2}}-\frac{\eta'}{s'^{2}}\frac{\,ds'}{\,ds}=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,ds'}{\,ds}=-\frac{\eta}{s^{2}}\cdot\frac{s'^{2}}{\eta'} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =- \left(\frac{\eta s'}{\eta' s}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{\eta'}{\eta}\right) \end{equation*}
using eqn. (5.2.24)
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad L= -m^{2}\left(\frac{\eta'}{\eta}\right) \end{equation*}
This represents the relation between lateral and longitudinal magnification. -ve sign indicates the image is laterally inverted.
