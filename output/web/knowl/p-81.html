Flux is the rate of flow of field normally through an imaginary surface. If \(\,dV\) is the volume of water that is flowing through a tube of cross-section \(A\) normally in time \(\,dt,\) then the flux of water through this tube is given by
\begin{equation*} \phi=\frac{\,dV}{\,dt}= \frac{A\,dx}{\,dt} = \vec{v}\cdot \vec{A} \end{equation*}
Where \(v\) is the velocity of flow of water in the tube. Electric flux can also be defined as number of electric field lines passing through any surface perpendicularly. Electric flux passes through an elementary surface area, \(\,dA\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \Phi_{E}= \int\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,dA} = \int E\cos\phi \,dA = \int E_{x}\,dA_{yz}+\int E_{y}\,dA_{xz}+\int E_{z}\,dA_{xy} \end{equation*}
