For a single slit,
\begin{equation*} y = A\int\limits_{-\frac{a}{2}}^{\frac{a}{2}}\sin\left(\omega t-kr+kz\sin\theta\right)\,dz \end{equation*}
Let \(\sin\left(\omega t -kr+kz\sin\theta\right) =\phi(z).\) Therefore, for N slits,
\begin{equation*} y = A\left[\int\limits_{-\frac{a}{2}}^{\frac{a}{2}}\phi(z)\,dz +\int\limits_{d-\frac{a}{2}}^{d+\frac{a}{2}}\phi(z)\,dz +\int\limits_{2d-\frac{a}{2}}^{2d+\frac{a}{2}}\phi(z)\,dz+\cdots+\int\limits_{(N-1)d-\frac{a}{2}}^{(N-1)d+\frac{a}{2}}\phi(z)\,dz \right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad y= \frac{Aa\sin\alpha}{\alpha}\left[\sin(\omega t -kr)+\sin(\omega t -kr+kd\sin\theta)+\sin(\omega t -kr+2kd\sin\theta)+\sin(\omega t -kr+(N-1)kd\sin\theta)\right] \tag{5.8.26} \end{equation}
\begin{equation*} \text{If}\quad S=\sin X+\sin (X+Y)+\sin (X+2Y)+\cdots+\sin (X+(N-1)Y) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{then,}\quad S\cdot\sin(\frac{Y}{2})=\sin X\cdot\sin(\frac{Y}{2})+\sin (X+Y)\cdot\sin(\frac{Y}{2})+\sin (X+2Y)\cdot\sin(\frac{Y}{2})+\cdots +\cdots+\sin (X+(N-1)Y)\cdot\sin(\frac{Y}{2}) \end{equation*}
Using the identity \(2sin(A)sin(B) = \cos(A-B)-\cos(A+B)\text{,}\) we would obtain,
\begin{equation*} S=\frac{\sin(X+(N-1)Y/2).\sin(NY/2)}{\sin(Y/2)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad y=\frac{Aa\sin\alpha}{\alpha}\left(\frac{\sin\frac{NY}{2}}{\sin\frac{Y}{2}}\right)\left[\sin\left\{X+\left(\frac{N-1}{2}\right)Y\right\}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} y=\frac{Aa\sin\alpha}{\alpha}\left(\frac{\sin N\beta}{\sin\beta}\right) \left[\sin\left\{\omega t -kr +\left(\frac{(N-1)kd\sin\theta}{2}\right)\right\}\right]\tag{5.8.27} \end{equation}
Therefore intensity at point P [FigureÂ 5.8.12.(a)]
\begin{equation} I = \frac{A^{2}a^{2}\sin^{2}\alpha}{\alpha^{2}}\left(\frac{\sin^{2} N\beta}{\sin^{2}\beta}\right) =I_{o}\left[\frac{\sin\alpha}{\alpha}\right]^{2}\left(\frac{\sin N\beta}{\sin\beta}\right)^{2} \tag{5.8.28} \end{equation}
Where \(I_{o}=A^{2}a^{2}\) is the maximum intensity at \(\alpha=0\) and \(\beta=0\text{.}\) The directions of principal maxima is given by \(N\beta=k\pi\text{,}\) where \(k=0,N,2N,3N,\cdots = nN\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad N\beta=\frac{Nkd\sin\theta}{2}= nN\pi \Rightarrow d\sin\theta=n\lambda \end{equation*}
where \(n=0,1,2,3,\cdots\) and \(k=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\text{.}\) If a is slit width and b is width of the opaque space, then
\begin{equation*} (a+b)\sin\theta_{n}=n\lambda \end{equation*}
provides the direction of principal maxima. The intensity goes to zero, when \(\frac{\sin\alpha}{\alpha}=0\) (diffraction condition) or \(\sin N\beta=0\) (interference term).
