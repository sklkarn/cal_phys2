The force acting on the string element along y-axis is given by
\begin{equation*} F_{y}= T\sin\theta+T\sin\theta=2 T\sin\theta = ma \end{equation*}
i.e., because of the circular portion of string each particle of string element experiences centripetal force.
\begin{equation*} \text{Hence,}\quad 2 T\sin\theta = m\frac{v^{2}}{R} \end{equation*}
where v is a velocity of wave which is passing through the string.
\begin{equation*} \text{Now,}\quad 2 T \theta = \mu R 2\theta \frac{v^{2}}{R} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad v= \sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}} \end{equation*}
Hence the velocity of wave depends on the tension and mass per unit length of a string. Velocity of mechanical waves generally takes the form
\begin{equation*} v=\frac{\text{Restoring ability of a system}}{\text{Inertia resisting the return to equilibrium}} \end{equation*}
