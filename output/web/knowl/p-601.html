Power is dc circuit is given by
\begin{equation*} P=VI \end{equation*}
as voltage and current in dc circuit is constant but in ac circuit they are changing with time hence in ac circuit instantaneous power dissipated can be determined as
\begin{equation*} P = VI = EI = E_{o}\sin(\omega t)\times I_{o}\sin(\omega t+\theta) \end{equation*}
where \(\theta\) is a phase difference between current and voltage.
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad P = E_{o}I_{o}\sin(\omega t)\sin(\omega t+\theta) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{E_{o}I_{o}}{2} \left[\cos(\omega t - \omega t - \theta)-\cos(\omega t+\omega t+\theta)\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad P = \frac{E_{o}I_{o}}{2} \left[\cos(- \theta)-\cos(2\omega t+\theta)\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad P =\frac{E_{o}I_{o}}{2}\left[\cos(\theta)-\cos(2\omega t+\theta)\right] \end{equation*}
\(\because\quad \cos(-\theta) =\cos\theta ;\) \(\sin\,A\sin\,B=\frac{1}{2}\left[\cos\,(A-B)-\cos\,(A+B)\right].\)
