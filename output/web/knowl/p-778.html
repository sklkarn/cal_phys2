If listener is moving towards a stationary source of sound with a velocity \(v_{l}\) then the relative velocity of sound is changed for the listener. Hence pitch of sound heard by the listener is given by
\begin{equation*} f_{l} = \frac{v'}{\lambda} =\frac{v+v_{l}}{\lambda} =\frac{v+v_{l}}{\frac{v}{f_{s}}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad f_{l}= \left(\frac{v+v_{l}}{v}\right)f_{s} \end{equation*}
The direction of sound velocity is taken as positive and hence if listener is moving towards the source then the relative velocity increased.
