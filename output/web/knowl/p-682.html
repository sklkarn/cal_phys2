Consider a vibrating spring with mass m attached to its end, then the kinetic energy of the mass
\begin{equation*} K=\frac{1}{2}mv^{2}, \end{equation*}
and potential energy stored in the spring with a displacement x is
\begin{equation*} U=\frac{1}{2}kx^{2}. \end{equation*}
Now since restoring force involved in spring mass system is conservative, so the total mechanical energy
\begin{equation*} E=K+U \end{equation*}
is conserved.
