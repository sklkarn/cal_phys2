Applying Kirchhoff’s II law to the loop BDCB,
\begin{equation} I_{g}G+(I_{2}+I_{g})X-(I_{1}-I_{g})Q=0 \tag{2.4.4} \end{equation}
When potential at junctions B and D are equal, then
\begin{equation*} I_{g}=0. \end{equation*}
Hence from eqns. (2.4.3) and (2.4.4), we get-
\begin{equation*} I_{1}P-I_{2}R=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad I_{1}P=I_{2}R \tag{2.4.5} \end{equation}
\begin{equation*} I_{2}X-I_{1}Q=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad I_{2}X=I_{1}Q\tag{2.4.6} \end{equation}
Dividing these equations, we get-
\begin{equation} \frac{P}{Q}=\frac{R}{X} \tag{2.4.7} \end{equation}
This is the required condition for balancing wheatstone’s bridge.
