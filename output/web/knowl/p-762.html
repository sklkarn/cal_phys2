Intensity level or loudness is measured in logarithmic scale as it depends upon human perception of sound, i.e
\begin{equation*} \beta = 10 \log\frac{I}{I_{o}} \quad (dB) \end{equation*}
The unit of loudness is decibels (dB) and the term \(I_{o} = 10^{-12} \,(W/m^{2})\) is a minimum intensity of sound that human ear can perceive with zero loudness. \(I_{o}\) is also known as Threshold of hearing as \(\beta=0\) at \(I=I_{o}\text{.}\)
