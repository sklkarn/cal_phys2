When a wave propagates through a medium it displaces the medium particles at rest from their mean (equilibrium) positions. Since the material of a medium has elastic property, surface tension, and has experienced the gravity the particles start experiencing a restoring to force which wants to bring them to their equilibrium position. According to Hookâ€™s law the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement of the particle from its mean position and directed along the mean position, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} F\propto y. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad F = -ky \end{equation*}
-ve sign indicates that the force F is opposite to the displacement y and k is called an elastic constant (or force constant) of the material of the medium.
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad m a = -ky \end{equation*}
where m is the mass of a oscillating particle.
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad a = \frac{\,d^{2}y}{\,dt^{2}}= -\frac{k}{m}y =-\omega^{2}y \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,d^{2}y}{\,dt^{2}}+\omega^{2}y=0 \end{equation*}
which is a differential form of SHM, and
\begin{equation*} \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} = 2\pi f \end{equation*}
is an angular frequency and \(f = \frac{1}{T}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad a=\frac{\,dv}{\,dt} = -\omega^{2}y \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,dv}{\,dx}\frac{\,dx}{\,dt} = -\omega^{2}y \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad v\frac{\,dv}{\,dx}= -\omega^{2}y \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \int\limits_{v_{o}}^{v} v \,dv = -\omega^{2}\int\limits_{y_{o}}^{y} y \,dy \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \left.\frac{v^{2}}{2}\right\vert_{v_{o}}^{v} = -\omega^{2}\left.\frac{y^{2}}{2}\right\vert_{y_{o}}^{y} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad v^{2}-v_{o}^{2}= -\omega^{2}\left(y^{2}-y_{o}^{2}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad v^{2}= \omega^{2}\left(\frac{v_{o}^{2}}{\omega^{2}}+y_{o}^{2}-y^{2}\right)=\omega^{2}\left(A^{2} - y^{2}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad v = \omega\sqrt{\left(A^{2}-y^{2}\right)} \tag{4.1.1} \end{equation}
where
\begin{equation*} A=\sqrt{\frac{v_{o}^{2}}{\omega^{2}}+y_{o}^{2}}, \end{equation*}
the amplitude or a maximum displacement of the particle.
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad v = \frac{\,dy}{\,dt}= \omega\sqrt{\left(A^{2}-y^{2}\right)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,dy}{\sqrt{\left(A^{2}-y^{2}\right)}}= \omega\,dt \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad \int\limits_{y_{o}}^{y}\frac{\,dy}{\sqrt{\left(A^{2}-y^{2}\right)}}= \omega \int\limits_{0}^{t}\,dt = \omega t \tag{4.1.2} \end{equation}
To solve eqn. (4.1.2), consider
\begin{equation*} \mathscr{I}= \int\frac{\,dy}{\sqrt{\left(A^{2}-y^{2}\right)}} \end{equation*}
