\begin{equation*} \tan\alpha =\frac{AB}{AO} =\frac{y}{s} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad\tan\alpha =\frac{A'B'}{A'O} =\frac{y'}{s'} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{y}{s} = \frac{y'}{s'} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \frac{y'}{y} = \frac{s'}{s} \tag{5.2.26} \end{equation}
Here y, y’, s, s’ are size of object, size of image, object distance, and image distance, respectively. Now from right angled \(\triangle^{s} A'B'F\) and \(POF\text{,}\) we have -
\begin{equation*} \tan\beta =\frac{A'B'}{A'F} =\frac{y'}{s'-f} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad\tan\beta =\frac{PO}{OF} =\frac{y}{f} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{y}{f} = \frac{y'}{s'-f} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \frac{y'}{y} = \frac{s'-f}{f} \tag{5.2.27} \end{equation}
From eqns. (5.2.26) and (5.2.27), we get -
\begin{equation*} \frac{s'}{s} = \frac{s'-f}{f} \quad\Rightarrow \quad s' f=ss'-sf \end{equation*}
Dividing by ss’f on both sides, we get-
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \frac{1}{s}+\frac{1}{s'} =\frac{1}{f} \tag{5.2.28} \end{equation}
Which is a lens formula. The lateral magnification produced by the lens is given by
\begin{equation} m =\frac{y'}{y} = -\frac{s'}{s} \tag{5.2.29} \end{equation}
-ve sign indicates an inverted image.
