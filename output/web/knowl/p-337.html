When switch k is connected to the point 2, capacitor statrs discharging. Applying Kirchhoff’s voltage law to the discharging loop, we have -
\begin{equation*} iR+\frac{q}{C}=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{q}{C} = -iR=-\frac{\,dq}{\,dt}R \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\,dq}{q}=-\frac{\,dt}{RC} \end{equation*}
Integrating, we get -
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \int\limits_{q_{o}}^{q(t)}\frac{\,dq}{q}=-\int\limits_{0}^{t}\frac{\,dt}{RC} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \left.ln q \right\vert_{q_{o}}^{q(t)} =-\frac{t}{RC} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad lnq(t)-lnq_{o}=-\frac{t}{RC} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad ln\frac{q(t)}{q_{o}}=-\frac{t}{RC} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{q(t)}{q_{o}}=e^{-(\frac{t}{RC})} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad q(t) = q_{o} e^{-(\frac{t}{RC})} \tag{2.4.11} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \text{also,}\quad V_{c}(t)=Ee^{-(\frac{t}{RC})} \tag{2.4.12} \end{equation}
Differentiating eqn. (2.4.11), we get-
\begin{equation} i(t)=-i_{o}e^{-(\frac{t}{RC})} \tag{2.4.13} \end{equation}
- ve sign indicates current is decreasing gradually during discharging of a capacitor. \(RC=\tau\) is called a time constant. The graph of charging and discharging of a capacitor is shown in Figure 2.4.5.(b). At one time constant potential of capacitors becomes \(63\%\) (emf of battery) during charging and \(36\%E\) during discharging.
