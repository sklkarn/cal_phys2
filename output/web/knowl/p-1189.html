Thus the secondary maxima are of decreasing intensity. For secondary minima, from eqn. (5.8.10),
\begin{equation*} \sin\theta_{n} = \frac{n\lambda}{a} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \alpha = \frac{ka}{2}\frac{n\lambda}{a} = n\pi \quad [k=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}] \tag{5.8.19} \end{equation}
Substituting \(n=1,2,3,\cdots\text{,}\) we have \(\alpha = \pi,\, 2\pi, \, 3\pi, \,\cdots\text{.}\) Therefore the intensity of secondary minima are given as
\begin{equation*} I=I_{o}\left(\frac{\sin\alpha}{\alpha}\right)^{2} =0 \end{equation*}
