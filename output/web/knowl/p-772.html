Consider two waves are interfering to each other, then the resultant wave is given by
\begin{equation*} s(x,t) = s_{a}(x,t)+s_{b}(x,t) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =s_{o}\sin(k_{a}x-\omega_{a} t)+s_{o}\sin(k_{b}x-\omega_{b} t) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 2s_{o}\sin\left[\left(\frac{k_{a}+k_{b}}{2}\right)x -\left(\frac{\omega_{a}+\omega_{b}}{2}\right)t\right] \cdot \cos\left[ \left(\frac{k_{a}-k_{b}}{2}\right)x -\left(\frac{\omega_{a}-\omega_{b}}{2}\right)t\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad s(t) = 2s_{o}\cos\left(\omega_{d}\right)t \sin\left(\omega_{c}\right)t \quad \text{at}\quad x=0 \end{equation*}
where \(\omega_{c}\) is the average of \(\omega_{a}\) and \(\omega_{b}\) and \(\omega_{d}\) is one half of their difference. The amplitude factor \(2s_{o}\cos\left(\omega_{d}\right)t\) varies slowly with \(\frac{\omega_{a} -\omega_{b}}{2}\text{.}\) The frequency of this second term is \(\frac{f_{a} -f_{b}}{2}\text{,}\) but the ear cannot hear the inversion of phase that occurs when phase is negative and the difference is small. This term is responsible for the beating effect, and is known as an envelope. The sine factor varies with a frequency
\begin{equation*} \omega_{d}=\frac{\omega_{a} +\omega_{b}}{2}. \end{equation*}
There are two times that the envelope (cosine term) passes through zero for every wavelength, so the beat frequency is twice the frequency of the envelope and is given by the magnitude of the difference of the two frequencies, i.e., beat frequency
\begin{equation*} f_{beat} = |f_{a}-f_{b}|. \end{equation*}
