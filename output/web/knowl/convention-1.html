Convention 4.6.3.

Sign Rules for Doppler Effect:
\begin{equation*} f_{L} = \frac{v+v_{L}}{v+v_{s}}f_{s} \quad \text{(sound)} \end{equation*}
\(v = +; \) \(v_{L}, \, v_{s} = + \quad \text{for}\quad L \oslash S. \quad [\text{L towards S}]\)
\begin{equation*} f_{R} = \sqrt{\frac{c-v}{c+v}} f_{s} \quad \text{(light)} \quad(c = +; v = + \quad \text{for}\quad R \oslash S) \end{equation*}
Remember: If a source is going away from a listener then denominator has to increase (i.e. \(v_{s}=+ve \)) to lower the pitch. If a source is coming towards a listener then denominator has to decrease (i.e. \(v_{s}=-ve\)) to raise the pitch.
