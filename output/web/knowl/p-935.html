Alternative: A thin lens is a combination of a number of thin prisms and as a refracting angle, A of a prism at the point of incidence of a ray in the lens is same, the angle of deviation is given as
\begin{equation*} \delta=A(\eta_{g}-1) \end{equation*}
which is constant irrespective of the angle of incidence for a ray incident on the lens. Now suppose a ray is incident at a height, h on a lens and produce a deviation \(\delta\text{.}\) Hence from a Figure 5.2.37.(a) we have -
\begin{equation*} \tan\delta = \frac{y}{f} \end{equation*}
Also from Figure 5.2.37.(b),
\begin{equation*} \delta = \alpha +\beta \end{equation*}
For a thin lens, \(\delta\) is small, hence are \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \tan\delta = \tan\alpha +\tan\beta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{y}{f}= \frac{y}{s} +\frac{y}{s'}; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{1}{f}= \frac{1}{s} +\frac{1}{s'} \end{equation*}
