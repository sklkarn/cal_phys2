\begin{equation*} \,dV= \,dV_{shell} = 4\pi x^{2}\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad Q = \int\,dq=\int\rho\,dV = \rho \int\limits_{r}^{R}4\pi x^{2}\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 4\pi\rho\left(\frac{x^{3}}{3}\right)_{r}^{R} = \frac{4\pi\rho}{3}(R^{3}-r^{3}) \end{equation*}
It is not possible to find electric field at any point in this case using Coulomb’s law so electric field inside the shell at any point P at a distance \(a\) from the center of hollow sphere can be found by using Gauss’s law
\begin{equation*} \phi=\mathop{\vcenter{\large\unicode{x222F}\,}}\vec{E}\cdot\vec{\,dA}= \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} E 4\pi a^{2}=\frac{1}{\epsilon_{o}}\int\limits_{0}^{a}\rho 4\pi x^{2}\,dx \end{equation*}
here \(a\) is radius of Gaussian surface and \(\rho (4\pi x^{2}\,dx)\) is the charge enclosed in Gaussian surface.
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad E = \frac{1}{\epsilon_{o}}\left[4\pi \left(\frac{x^{3}}{3}\right)_{0}^{a}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad E(4\pi a^{2})= \frac{1}{\epsilon_{o}}\frac{4\pi}{3}a^{3} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E= \frac{a}{3\epsilon_{o}} \end{equation*}
