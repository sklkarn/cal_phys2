The laws of refraction state that
  1. The incident ray, the normal at the point of incidence, and the refracted ray are coplanar;
  2. The ratio of sine of the angles of incidence to the sine of angle of refraction is equal to the ratio of refractive indices of the second and first medium, i.e.
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\sin \theta_{i}}{\sin \theta_{r}}= \frac{\eta_{r}}{\eta_{i}}= {}^{i}{\eta_{r}}. \end{equation*}
where \(\eta{i}\) and \(\eta{r}\) are absolute refractive index of first and second medium, respectively. An absolute refractive index is the measure of refractive index of the medium with respect to air or vacuum. \({}^{i}{\eta_{r}}\) is read as refractive index of a second medium with respect to the first.
