Consider a disc of radius \(R\) having total charge \(Q\) and uniform surface charge density \(\sigma. \) To find the field at point \(P\) consider an elementary charged ring of radius \(r,\) and thickness \(\,dr \) having charge \(\,dq \) then the field at point \(P\) due to a ring is given by the eqn. (1.3.1), i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \,dE = \,dE_{x}= \frac{k x \,dq}{(x^{2}+r^{2})^3/2}\quad \text{(along x-axis)}. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E = E_{x} =\int \,dE_{x} =\int\limits_{0}^{R}\frac{k x (\sigma 2\pi r\,dr)}{(x^{2}+r^{2})^3/2} \end{equation*}
\([\because \,dq = \sigma \) (area of the ring)].
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad E = 2\pi\sigma k x \int\limits_{0}^{R}\frac{r\,dr}{(x^{2}+r^{2})^3/2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{Now let}\quad \mathscr{I}= \int \frac{r\,dr}{(x^{2}+r^{2})^{3/2}} \end{equation*}
From geometry of the figure \(x^{2}+r^{2}=t^{2}\)
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad 2r\,dr=2t\,dt \quad \text{or,} \quad r\,dr=t\,dt \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \mathscr{I}= \int \frac{t\,dt}{t^{3}} = \int t^{-2}\,dt = -\frac{1}{t} = \left(-\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}+r^{2}}}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E = 2\pi\sigma k x \left(-\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}+r^{2}}}\right)_{0}^{R} = -2\pi\sigma k x \left[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}+R^{2}}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}+0}}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =2\pi\sigma k x \left[\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}+R^{2}}}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad E=2\pi\sigma k \left[1-\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^{2}+R^{2}}}\right] \end{equation*}
If \(x=0\) the field at the center of the disc (or, field near charged surface, \(E=\frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_{o}}\))
\begin{equation*} or,\quad E =2\pi\sigma k = \frac{kQ}{R^{2}}\quad \text{(along x-axis).} \end{equation*}
\([\sigma=\frac{Q}{\pi R^{2}}]\) If \(x \lt \lt R,\)
\begin{equation*} E=2\pi\sigma k [1-\frac{x}{R}]= 2\pi\sigma k \end{equation*}
If \(x \gt \gt R,\)
\begin{equation*} E=2\pi\sigma k [1-\frac{x}{x}]= 0 \end{equation*}
