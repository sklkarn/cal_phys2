A copper wire of diameter 1 mm is 100 m long and has a potential difference of 12 V between its ends. Find
  1. the resistance of the wire,
  2. the current in the wire,
  3. the current density in the wire,
  4. the electric field in the wire,
  5. the concentration of free electrons (assuming one electron per copper atom),
  6. the drift velocity of the electrons in the wire, and
  7. the amount of electrical charge flowing through the wire in one minute.
Note: \(\rho_{cu} = 1.72\times10^{-8} \,\Omega.m,\) \(D_{cu} = 8.9 \,g/cm^{3},\quad M_{cu} = 63.546 \,g/mole, \) \(N_{A} = 6.022\times10^{23}\) atom/mole.
