From eqn. (5.8.1), it is clear that intensity is \(4a^{2}\) at bright point and zero at dark point. Hence the energy is transferred from dark point to bright point. The intensity of the bright points due to two waves without interference would be \(2a^{2}\) which is the same as the average intensity after interference \(\frac{4a^{2}+0}{2}=2a^{2}\) (FigureÂ 5.8.3.(b)). The waves emitting from the sources \(S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\) are in phase hence, whether the point P is bright or dark depends upon the path difference of these waves when reach to point P. The path difference between waves at point P is given by
\begin{equation*} \Delta x = S_{2}L= S_{2}P-S_{1}P \end{equation*}
Since \(S_{1}P =LP\) as \(S_{1}L\) is a perpendicular on \(S_{2}P\text{.}\) The waves travel the same distance from \(S_{1}\) to P and from L to P, hence, whether the waves reach at point P are in phase or in opposite phase can be described by \(\Delta x\text{.}\) Where
\begin{equation*} \Delta x = r_{2}-r_{1}=d\sin\theta \end{equation*}
from \(\triangle S_{1}S_{2}L.\)
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \theta = \frac{\Delta x}{d} \tag{5.8.2} \end{equation}
The slits \(S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\) are very close to each other hence angle \(\theta\) is very small. The screen is placed very far away from the slits hence the waves from \(S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\) to P can be considered as parallel to each other, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta = \frac{OP}{CO} =\frac{y}{D} \end{equation*}
from \(\triangle PCO.\)
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \theta =\frac{y}{D} \tag{5.8.3} \end{equation}
From eqns. (5.8.2) and (5.8.3), we have -
\begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta x}{d} = \frac{y}{D} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad \Delta x = \frac{yd}{D} \tag{5.8.4} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad \delta = \left(\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\right)\Delta x =\left(\frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \right) \left(\frac{yd}{D} \right) \tag{5.8.5} \end{equation}
