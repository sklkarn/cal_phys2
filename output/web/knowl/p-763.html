A decibel is one tenth of a bel, \(B\text{.}\) The unit bel is taken to give honor to a telephone inventor Alexendor Graham Bell. Since Bel is an inconveniently large unit so we use decibel. A typical conversation occurs at 60 to 70 dB pleasant to hear. A sound from nearby storm is 120 dB may cause severe damage to the eardrum. Intensity level follows the inverse square law. That is
\begin{equation*} I \propto \frac{1}{r^{2}}; \end{equation*}
\(\left[\because I = \frac{P}{A}=\frac{P}{4\pi r^{2}}\right]\) where r is the distance from the source.
