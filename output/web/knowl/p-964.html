From eqns. (5.3.2) and (5.3.3)
\begin{equation*} \theta = \delta_{b}-\delta_{r}=A(\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}) \end{equation*}
Dividing this expression by eqn. (5.3.1), we get -
\begin{equation} \frac{\theta}{\delta}=\frac{\delta_{b}-\delta_{r}}{\delta}=\frac{\eta_{b}-\eta_{r}}{\eta -1}= \frac{\,d\eta}{\eta -1}=\omega \tag{5.3.4} \end{equation}
The ratio of angular dispersion between violet and red emergent rays and the deviation suffered by the mean ray is known as the dispersive power of the material of a prism. It is constant for any two colors (wavelengths).
