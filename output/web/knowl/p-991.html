\begin{equation*} \frac{HQ}{QE} = \frac{MQ}{MQ}\quad \Rightarrow HQ = QE \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad HE=HE+QE= 2QE \tag{5.4.1} \end{equation}
Similarly, from similar \(\triangle^{s} ENR\) and \(RNF\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad EF=ER+RF= 2ER \tag{5.4.2} \end{equation}
From eqns. (5.4.1) and (5.4.2), we get -
\begin{equation} \text{or,}\quad HF=HE+EF= 2QE + 2ER = 2(QE+ER) \tag{5.4.3} \end{equation}
If EP is drawn perpendicular on MN from E, then the lines MQ, PE, and NR are all parallel to each other, hence,
\begin{equation*} MP=QE\qquad \text{also}\quad PN=ER \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\qquad HF = 2(QE+ER) = 2(MP+PN) = 2MN \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} or, \quad MN= \frac{1}{2}HF\tag{5.4.4} \end{equation}
Hence, the size of mirror, MN must be half the size of a person, HF.
