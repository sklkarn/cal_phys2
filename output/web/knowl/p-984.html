If \(a \lt \lt \lambda\text{,}\) than the amount of light scattered is given by
\begin{equation*} \sigma \propto \frac{1}{\lambda^{4}}. \end{equation*}
When sunlight interacts with atmospheric particles, blue colors scattered more in sky than any other colors from the visible spectrum of light, hence, the sky looks bluish in color. In fact, violet light scattered more than blue but our eyes are more sensitive to blue color than violet light hence blue colors are much more prevailed than others in sky. At sunset and sunrise, the sunlight has to travel longer distance in the atmosphere which causes most of the blue and other shorter wavelengths are scattered in the sky and only red color is able to reach to our eyes hence, the sun appears reddish in color.
