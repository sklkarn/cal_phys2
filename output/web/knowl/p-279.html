If \(\,dq \) amount of charges passes through any cross section, \(s\) of a conductor in time \(\,dt\) then electric current \(\,dI\) through that conductor is given by
\begin{equation*} I=\frac{\,dq}{\,dt} \end{equation*}
and current \(\,dI\) passes through an infinitesimal area \(\,dA\) normally is called a current density, \(J\),
\begin{equation} J=\frac{\,dI}{\,dA} \tag{2.0.2} \end{equation}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \,dI=J.\,dA \end{equation*}
