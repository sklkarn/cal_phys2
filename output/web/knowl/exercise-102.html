Exercise 27.

An alternator consists of a rectangular coil of length, L = 20 cm and width, W = 10 cm with 500 turns. At time t = 0 it is in the same horizontal plane as a constant magnetic field B = 20 T as shown in figure below. It rotates at a constant rate of 1000 rpm. The output is applied to a resistor of \(100 \,\Omega.\)
  1. What is the angular velocity \(\omega\) as a function of time?
  2. What is the angle \(\theta\) between the magnetic field vector \(\vec{B} \) and the area vector \(\vec{A}\) as a function of time?
  3. What is the magnetic flux through the coil as a function of time?
  4. What is the emf induced in the coil as a function of time?
  5. What is the current through the resistor as a function of time?
  6. What is the voltage at point a with respect to point b when t = 10 ms?
