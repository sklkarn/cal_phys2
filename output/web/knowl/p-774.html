If source is moving towards listener with a velocity \(v_{s}\) then listener receives more waves everysecond causes decrease in wavelength. Hence pitch of sound heard by the listener is given by
\begin{equation*} f_{l} = \frac{v}{\lambda'} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{v}{\lambda -v_{s}T} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{v}{\frac{v}{f_{s}} -\frac{v_{s}}{f_{s}}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \left(\frac{v}{v-v_{s}}\right)f_{s} \end{equation*}
When source is moving towards the listener wavelength squeezed by \(\lambda-v_{s}T\) for every cycle of wave. Here T is a time period of the wave.
