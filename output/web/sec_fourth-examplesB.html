    Section 4.7 Examples B

    Example 4.7.1.

    A mass of 2 kg is suspended by a string of length 2 m and mass 100 g. Find
    1. the speed of a transverse wave in the string,
    2. the fundamental frequency of the string (assuming the wavelength is twice the length of the string),
    3. the power transferred up and down the string while vibrating in its fundamental frequency with an amplitude of 2 cm.
    Solution.
    Given: \(M =2\,kg, \quad L =2\,m, \quad m=0.1\,kg, \quad\lambda = 4\,m,\) \(A = 0.02\,m,\quad g=9.8 \,m/s^{2}\)
    1. \begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{F}{\mu}}, \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} \quad F = Mg; \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} \mu = \frac{m}{L} \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} \mu = 0.05 \,kg/m;\quad F = 19.6 \,N;\quad v = 9.8 \,m/s \end{equation*}
    2. \begin{equation*} f= \frac{v}{\lambda}=\frac{v}{2L}= 4.9 \,Hz \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} \omega = 2\pi f = 31.1 \,rad/s \end{equation*}
    3. \(\displaystyle P_{avg} = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\mu F}\omega^{2}A^{2} \Rightarrow P_{avg} = 0.192 \,W\)

    Example 4.7.2.

    At what temperature in Celsius is the speed of sound in air equal to \(345 \,m/s\text{.}\) Assume that air is an ideal gas with a molecular mass of \(28.8 \,g/mole\) (slightly more than that of nitrogen) and that air is diatomic with \(\gamma = 1.4.\)
    Solution.
    Given: \(v=345 \,m/s,\quad M = 28.8\times10^{-3}\,kg/mol,\) \(\gamma = 1.4,\quad R=8.314 \,J/mol/K\)
    \begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{\gamma R T }{M}}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} T_{c} = T_{K} -273.15 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \Rightarrow T = 294.5\,K;\quad \therefore \quad T_{c} =21.3 \,^{o}C \end{equation*}

    Example 4.7.3.

    A string of mass 100 g and length 2 m is under a tension of 200 N. Find its
    1. wave velocity,
    2. fundamental frequency,
    3. \(3^{rd} \) harmonic frequency, and
    4. \(5^{th}\) overtone wavelength.
    Solution.
    Given: \(m=0.1\,kg,\quad L=2\,m, \quad F = 200\,N, \quad x = 0.02\,m\)
    1. \begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{F}{\mu}};\quad \mu = \frac{m}{L}=0.05 \,kg/m\quad \Rightarrow v= 63.25 \,m/s \end{equation*}
    2. \begin{equation*} v = f_{o}\lambda_{o} \Rightarrow f_{o}= \frac{v}{\lambda_{o}} = \frac{v}{2L}= 15.8 \,Hz \end{equation*}
    3. \begin{equation*} f_{3} =3f_{o} \Rightarrow f_{3}= 47.4 \,Hz \end{equation*}
    4. \begin{equation*} f_{6} =6f_{o} =\frac{v}{\lambda_{6}} \Rightarrow\lambda_{6}= \frac{v}{f_{6}} = 0.667 \,m \end{equation*}
      or, \(NANANANANANAN \Rightarrow L = 12 (L_{NA}) =12\left(\frac{1}{4}\lambda_{6}\right) \)

    Example 4.7.4.

    Two omnidirectional speakers are located at points (0, 1 m) and (0, -2 m), respectively. What is the minimum frequency of a sound wave that would produce constructive interference at the point (5 m, 0)?
    Solution.
    Given: \(y_{1} =1\,m,\quad y_{2} =2\,m,\quad x=5\,m, \quad v=345 \,m/s\)
    \begin{equation*} r_{1}=\sqrt{x^{2}+y_{1}^{2}}=5.099\, m; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} r_{2}=\sqrt{x^{2}+y_{2}^{2}} =5.385 \,m \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} r_{2}-r_{1} = \lambda_{o} =0.286 \,m \quad\text{for minimum frequency} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} v = f \lambda_{o} \quad \therefore \quad f=1206 \,Hz \end{equation*}

    Example 4.7.5.

    The pressure amplitude of a \(500\, H_{z}\) sound wave is \(2\times10^{-2} \,Pa.\) If the Bulk modulus of air is \(1.42\times10^{5} \,Pa\) and the density of air is \(1.20 \,kg/m^{3},\) find
    1. the speed of sound in the air,
    2. the displacement amplitude of the wave,
    3. the intensity of the wave,
    4. the intensity level of the sound.
    Solution.
    Given: \(f=500 \,H_{z}; \quad p_{max}=2\times10^{-2} \,Pa;\) \(B = 1.42\times10^{5}\, Pa; \quad \rho = 1.20 \,kg/m^{2};\) \(I_{o} =10^{-12} \,W/m^{2}\)
    Equations:
    \begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}};\ \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \omega = 2\pi f; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} v = \frac{\omega}{k}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} p_{max} = BkA; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} I = \frac{p_{max}}{2\rho v}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \beta = 10 log\frac{I}{I_{o}} \end{equation*}
    1. \begin{equation*} \omega =3142\, rad/s \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} k=9.13 \,/m \end{equation*}
      \(\therefore \, v= 344 \,m/s\)
    2. \(\displaystyle A = 1.54\times10^{-8} \,m \)
    3. \(\displaystyle I = 4.85\times10^{-7} \,W/m^{2}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \beta = 57\, dB\)

    Example 4.7.6.

    A 100 W speaker has a diameter of 30 cm (about 1 ft).
    1. What is the maximum intensity level it can produce.
    2. If the speaker were placed in the middle of a vast desert, how far away could it be heard?
    Solution.
    Given: \(P = 100 \,W; \quad D = 0.3 \,m;\quad I_{o} = 10^{-12} \,W/m^{2} \)
    Equations:
    \begin{equation*} A =\pi r^{2}=\pi \frac{D^{2}}{4}\quad \text{(speaker area.)}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} I =\frac{P}{A}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \beta = 10 \log\frac{I}{I_{o}}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} I_{o} = \frac{P}{A_{2}}; \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} A_{2}=4\pi r^{2}\quad \text{(spherical surface occupied by sound.)} \end{equation*}
    1. \begin{equation*} A=0.0707 \,m^{2}; \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} I=1415 \,W/m^{2}; \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} \therefore \beta= 152 \,dB \end{equation*}
    2. \begin{equation*} A_{2} =1.00\times10^{14}\, m^{2}; \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} \therefore r= 2.82\times10^{3} \,km \end{equation*}

    Example 4.7.7.

    A sound meter 2.0 m from a radio registers 75 dB coming from it. What would an identical meter 400 m away read from the radio?
    Solution.
    \begin{equation*} \beta=10\log\frac{I}{I_{o}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \beta_{2}-\beta_{1} = 10\left[\log\frac{I_{2}}{I_{o}}-\log\frac{I_{1}}{I_{o}}\right] = 10\log\frac{I_{2}}{I_{1}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \beta_{2}=\beta_{1} + 10 \log\left(\frac{r_{1}^{2}}{r_{2}^{2}}\right) = \beta_{1}+ 20\log\frac{r_{1}}{r_{2}} = 28.97 \,dB \end{equation*}

    Example 4.7.8.

    A car traveling 20 m/s blows its horn of frequency m 300 H_{z} as it passes a second car at rest. If the speed of sound in air is 345 m/s, find
    1. the frequency hear by the second car before the first passes it,
    2. the frequency heard afterwards,
    3. the wavelength of the sound ahead of the first car,
    4. the wavelength behind the first car.
    5. If the second car blows its horn after the first passes, what is the beat frequency heard by the second car?
    6. By the first car?
    Solution.
    Given: \(v_{s} = 20\, m/s, \quad f_{s} =300 \,Hz, \quad v_{L} =0 \)
    1. \begin{equation*} f_{L} = \frac{v+v_{L}}{v+v_{s}}f_{s} = \left(\frac{345+0}{345+(-20)}\right)300 = 318.5 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}
    2. \begin{equation*} f_{L} = \frac{v+v_{L}}{v+v_{s}}f_{s} = \left( \frac{345+0}{345+(20)}\right)300 = 284.4 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}
    3. \begin{equation*} v = f\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda = \frac{v}{f} =\frac{345}{318.5} = 1.083\, m \end{equation*}
    4. \begin{equation*} v = f\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda = \frac{v}{f} =\frac{345}{284.4} = 1.213 \,m \end{equation*}
    5. \begin{equation*} f_{beat} = \Delta f = f_{a} -f_{b} = 300-284.4 = 15.6 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}
    6. \begin{equation*} f_{L} = \frac{v+v_{L}}{v+v_{s}}f_{s} = \left(\frac{345+(-20)}{345+0}\right)300 = 282.6 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} f_{beat} = \Delta f = f_{a} -f_{b} = 300-282.6 = 17.4 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}

    Example 4.7.9.

    A student is skating at a speed of 3 m/s toward the wall of a large building. When he is 100 m from the building he blows a \(500 H_{z}\) whistle and listens for the echo.
    1. How far does he travel before hearing the echo?
    2. What is the echo frequency he hears?
    3. What is the beat frequency between the echo and his whistle? Assume the speed of sound in air is 345 m/s.
    Solution.
    1. \begin{equation*} 2D = vt+v_{2}t \quad\Rightarrow \quad 2 (100 ) = 345 t + 3 t \quad \Rightarrow\quad t = 0.5747 \,s \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} x =v_{2}t =(3) (0.5747) = 1.72 \,m \end{equation*}
    2. \begin{equation*} f_{wall} =\frac{v+v_{wall}}{v+v_{s}}f_{s} = \frac{345+0}{345+(-3)}500 = 504.4 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} f_{L} =\frac{v+v_{L}}{v+v_{wall}}f_{wall} = \frac{345+3}{345+(0)}504.4 = 508.8 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}
    3. \begin{equation*} f_{beat} = \Delta f = f_{L} -f_{s} = 508.8 - 500 = 8.8 \,H_{z} \end{equation*}

    Example 4.7.10.

    The first two spectral lines of the Balmer series for Hydrogen gas are the \(H_{\alpha}\) and \(H_{\beta}\) lines with wavelengths 656.3 nm and 486.1 nm, respectively.
    1. How fast is a galaxy moving away from us if its \(H_{\beta}\) line is shifted all the way to the \(H_{\alpha}\) wavelength?
    2. What is the fractional change in wavelength of a star‚Äôs spectrum if the star is moving away from us at half the speed of light?
    3. What will be the observed shift in wavelength of the \(H_{\alpha}\) line for a star moving toward the earth at a speed equal to that of the earth around the sun? The radius of the earth‚Äôs orbit is \(1.50\times10^{11}\, m.\)
    Solution.
    Given: \(\lambda_{s} = 4.861\times10^{-7} \,m\) \(\lambda_{R} = 6.563\times10^{-7} \,m\) \(c= 2.998\times10^{8} \,m/s\) \(v = 0.5 \,c\) \(r = 1.50\times10^{11}\, m\) \(t = 1 \,year = 3.156\times10^{7} \,s\) \(\lambda_{s} = 6.563\times10^{-7} \,m\)
    Equations:
    \begin{equation*} c = f_{s}\lambda_{s} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} c = f_{R}\lambda_{R} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} f_{R} = \sqrt{\frac{c-v}{c+v}} f_{s} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\lambda_{R}}{\lambda_{s}} = \frac{f_{s}}{f_{R}} = \sqrt{\frac{c+v}{c-v}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda_{s}} = \frac{\lambda_{R}-\lambda_{s}}{\lambda_{s}} =\frac{\lambda_{R}}{\lambda_{s}}-1 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} 2\pi r = v t \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda_{s}} =\sqrt{ \frac{c+v}{c-v}}-1 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\lambda_{s}}{\lambda_{R}} = \frac{f_{R}}{f_{L}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} v = \sqrt{\frac{1-\left(\frac{\lambda_{s}}{\lambda_{R}}\right)^{2}}{1+\left(\frac{\lambda_{s}}{\lambda_{R}}\right)^{2}}} c = 0.291 \,c \end{equation*}
    1. \(v= 8.74\times10^{7} \,m/s\)
      \begin{equation*} \frac{\lambda_{R}}{\lambda_{s}}=1.732 \end{equation*}
    2. \begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda_{s}}=0.732 \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} v= 2.987\times10^{4} \,m/s \end{equation*}
      \begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda_{s}}=\frac{v}{c} \end{equation*}
      for \(\frac{v}{c} \lt \lt 1.\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \Delta\lambda = 6.539\times10^{11} \,m\)
