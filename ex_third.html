    3.

    A small particle of mass 2 g and charge \(10 \,\mu C\) is passes through the origin with a velocity of \(2 \,m/s\) in the +x direction into a uniform magnetic field parallel to the z-axis and a uniform gravitational field of 1 gauss in the -y direction.
    1. If there is no electrical field and the particle continues moving in a straight line at constant speed, what is the magnetic force on the particle?
    2. What is the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field?
    3. If the particle attained its speed by sliding down a ramp before entering the magnetic field, what was the height of the ramp?

    4.

    A electron cyclotron has a frequency of \(2.4 \,MH_{z}\) and an orbital radius of 1 m.
    1. What is its magnetic field strength?
    2. What is the speed of the electron?
    3. What was the voltage on the electron gun which projected the electron into the cyclotron?
    Hint.
    \(m_{e} = 9.11\times10^{-31} \,kg, \quad e = 1.6\times10^{-19} \,C.\)

    \begin{equation*} \vec{B} = 5 \,T \hat{i}. \end{equation*}
    1. What is the magnetic flux across the surface abcd?
    2. Through cdef?
    3. Through abfe?
    4. Through all five surfaces enclosing the volume?

    7.

    An alpha particle (nucleus of a helium atom) has a speed of \(3\times10^{6} \,m/s\) and moves in a circle of radius 10 cm in the x/y-plane due to a uniform magnetic field.
    1. What is the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field?
    2. What is the cyclotron frequency of the alpha particle?
    3. Through what potential difference was it accelerated?
    4. What is its energy in units of electron-volts?

    Mag. Force.

    8.

    A wire 2 m long lies along the y-axis and carries a current of 2 A in the +y direction. What is the magnitude and direction of the magnetic force on the wire if it lies in a uniform magnetic field of
    1. \(\displaystyle \vec{B} = 1 \,T \hat{i},\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \vec{B} = - 2 \,T \hat{j},\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \vec{B} = 2 \,T \hat{j}+ 3 \,T \hat{k}, \)
    4. \(\displaystyle \vec{B} = 1 \,T \hat{i}- 2\, T \hat{j}+ 3 \,T \hat{k},\)

    9.

    An electromagnetic rail gun consists of two parallel conducting rails of length X = 1000 m spanned by a sliding bar of mass m = 2 kg, length L = 1 m, and resistance \(10\Omega \) as shown in the figure below. A constant electrical potential V = 100 V is maintained between the rails and a constant magnetic field B = 10 T is maintained between them.
    1. What is the magnitude and direction of the current in the sliding bar?
    2. What is the magnitude and direction of the magnetic force on the sliding bar?
    3. Neglecting friction, what is the acceleration of the bar?
    4. Starting from rest at one end of the rail gun, what is the speed of the bar when it reaches the other end?
    5. What is the kinetic energy of the bar when it leaves the rail gun?
    6. How long a time is the bar being accelerated?
    7. How much electrical energy is supplied by the battery during this time interval?
    8. Why aren’t answers g and e the same?

    10.

    A square wire loop hangs vertically below the horizontal z-axis as shown in the figure below. A uniform magnetic field parallel to the y-axis deflects the wire from its vertical position until it attains equilibrium with the force of gravity. If the length of the square is 10 cm, the current through the wire is 2 A, the deflection angle is \(30^{o},\) and the magnetic field strength is 0.1 T, find
    1. the magnetic moment of the loop,
    2. the magnetic torque on the loop when it is vertical,
    3. the magnetic torque on the loop when it is tilted,
    4. the mass of the loop, and
    5. the total force exerted by the loop on the hinge along the z-axis when it is in tilted equilibrium.

    11.

    A solenoid is used as the rotor of an electrical motor. It is 10 cm long and 2 cm in diameter. The wire wrapped around it makes 1000 turns and has a resistance of \(100 \,\Omega\text{.}\) The permanent magnets in the motor create a uniform magnetic field of 1000 G and the voltage driving the motor is 12 Volt DC. If the motor runs at 120 rpm, find
    1. the current in the wire,
    2. the magnetic moment of the solenoid,
    3. the magnetic potential energy when the magnetic moment is opposite the field,
    4. the change in magnetic potential energy for each half rotation,
    5. the magnetic power delivered by the motor,
    6. the electrical power delivered by the battery,
    7. the electrical power dissipated by the resistor in the form of heat.
    8. Which of these answers would change if the motor rotated faster?
    9. Are your answers consistent with the law of conservation of energy? Explain.

    12.

    A proton of mass \(1.67\times10^{-27} \,kg\) and charge \(1.6\times10^{-19} \,C\) is moving in the +x direction with a constant speed of 104 m/s in a region of space containing a constant electric field and a constant magnetic field. If the magnetic field is 10 T in the +y direction,
    1. what is the magnitude and direction of the electric field that will keep the particle moving in a straight line at constant speed?
    2. Describe the subsequent motion of the particle if the electric field is suddenly turned off.
    3. What is the maximum distance the proton would deviate from its initial position when the electric field is turned off?

    15.

    Given a circular loop of current I with a radius a.
    1. Show that the magnetic field at a distance x along the axis of this loop is given by
      \begin{equation*} B = \frac{\mu_{o}Ia^{2}}{2(x^{2}+a^{2})^{3/2}}. \end{equation*}
    2. Use this result to determine the magnetic field along the axis of a coil of N turns.
    3. Use the result of part a to determine the magnetic field at the center of a single loop of current.
    4. What is the magnetic field at the center of a loop of current 10 cm in radius carrying a current of 2 A?

    16.

    A long straight wire lies along the x-axis carrying 2 A of current in the +x direction. A second, parallel wire located at y = 20 cm carries current of 5 A in the opposite direction.
    1. What is the magnetic field due to these currents at the point (0, 0.1 m)?
    2. At the point (1m, 1m)?
    3. What is the magnitude and direction of the magnetic force on a \(10 \mu C\) charge moving in the +z direction with a speed of \(10^{4} \,m/s \) if it is at the point (-0.2 m, -0.2 m)?

    17.

    1. (1m, 0, 2m),
    2. (2m, 1m, 0),
    3. (0, 1m, -2m).
